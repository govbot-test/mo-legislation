4 "304.822. 1. This section shall be known as the "Siddens Bening Hands Free Law".
5 2. As used in this section, the following terms shall mean:
6 (1) "Commercial motor vehicle", the same meaning as is ascribed to such term in section
7 302.700;
8 (2) "Electronic communication device", a portable device that is used to initiate, receive,
9 store, or view communication, information, images, or data electronically.
10 (a) Such term shall include but not be limited to: cellular telephones; portable
11 telephones; text-messaging devices; personal digital assistants; pagers; broadband personal
12 communication devices; electronic devices with mobile data access; computers, including but
13 not limited to tablets, laptops, notebook computers, and electronic or video game systems;
14 devices capable of transmitting, retrieving, or displaying a video, movie, broadcast television
15 image, or visual image; and any substantially similar device that is used to initiate or receive
16 communication or store and review information, videos, images, or data.
17 (b) Such term shall not include: radios; citizens band radios; commercial two-way radio
18 communication devices or their functional equivalent; subscription-based emergency
19 communication devices; prescribed medical devices; amateur or ham radio devices; or global
20 positioning system receivers, security, navigation, communication, or remote diagnostics
21 systems permanently affixed to the vehicle;
22 (3) "Highway", the same meaning as is ascribed to such term in section 302.010;
23 (4) "Noncommercial motor vehicle", the same meaning as is ascribed to such term in
24 section 302.700;
25 (5) "Operating", the actual physical control of a vehicle;
26 (6) "Operator", a person who is in actual physical control;
27 (7) "School bus", the same meaning as is ascribed to such term in section 302.700;
28 (8) "Voice-operated or hands-free feature or function", a feature or function, whether
29 internally installed or externally attached or connected to an electronic communication device,
30 that allows a person to use an electronic communication device without the use of either hand,

3 3. Except as otherwise provided in this section, while operating a noncommercial motor
4 vehicle or commercial motor vehicle on any highway or property open to the public for vehicular

6 (1) Physically hold or support, with any part of his or her body, an electronic

8 (2) Write, send, or read any text-based communication, including but not limited to a
9 text message, instant message, email, or social media interaction on an electronic communication
10 device. This subdivision shall not apply to operators of a noncommercial motor vehicle using a
11 voice-operated or hands-free feature or function that converts the message to be sent as a
12 message in a written form, provided that the operator does not divert his or her attention from
13 lawful operation of the vehicle;
14 (3) Make any communication on an electronic communication device, including a phone
15 call, voice message, or one-way voice communication; provided however, that this prohibition
16 shall not apply to use of a voice-operated or hands-free feature or function;
17 (4) Engage in any form of electronic data retrieval or electronic data communication on
18 an electronic communication device;
19 (5) Manually enter letters, numbers, or symbols into any website, search engine, or
20 application on an electronic communication device;
21 (6) Watch a video or movie on an electronic communication device, other than watching
22 data related to the navigation of the vehicle; or
23 (7) Record, post, send, or broadcast video, including a video conference, on an electronic
24 communication device, provided that this prohibition shall not apply to electronic devices used
25 for the sole purpose of continually monitoring operator behavior by recording or broadcasting
26 video within or outside the vehicle.
27 4. The operator of a school bus shall not use or operate an electronic communication
28 device while the school bus is in motion unless the device is being used in a similar manner as a
29 two-way radio to allow live communication between the operator and school officials or public
30 safety officials. The operator of a school bus shall not use or operate an electronic
31 communication device or a two-way radio while loading or unloading passengers.
32 5. This section shall not apply to:

36 (2) Operators using an electronic communication device for the sole purpose of reporting
37 an emergency situation and continuing communication with emergency personnel during the
38 emergency situation;
