1 AMEND House Committee Substitute for House Bill No. 2742, Page 1, Section 160.3300, Line
2 1, by inserting after the number "160.3300." the number "1."; and
3
4 Further amend said bill, page, and section, Line 9, by inserting after all of said line the
5 following:
6
7 "2. No image or video captured by a school bus safety camera authorized under this
8 section shall be used by a political subdivision for violation detection or enforcement as part of
9 any automated camera system designed to detect traffic violations and issue citations. Nothing
10 in this section shall prohibit a prosecutor from introducing any image or video captured by a
11 school bus safety camera as evidence in a judicial proceeding."; and
12
13 Further amend said bill, Pages 1-4, Section 302.302, Lines 1-108, by deleting all of said section
14 and lines from the bill; and
15
16 Further amend said bill, Pages 4-6, Section 304.070, Lines 1-43, by deleting all of said lines and
17 inserting in lieu thereof the following:
18
19 "304.070. 1. Any person who violates any of the provisions of subsections 1, 3, and 7 of
20 section 304.050 is guilty of a class A misdemeanor. In addition, the court may suspend the
21 driver's license of any person who violates the provision of subsection 1 of section 304.050. If
22 ordered by the court, the director shall suspend the driver's license for ninety days for a first
23 offense of subsection 1 of section 304.050, and one hundred twenty days for a second or
24 subsequent offense of subsection 1 of section 304.050. Any person who violates subsection 1 of
25 section 304.050 where such violation results in the physical injury of any child shall be guilty of
26 a class E felony. Any person who violates subsection 1 of section 304.050 where such violation
27 causes the serious physical injury or death of any child shall be guilty of a class D felony. For
28 the purposes of this subsection, “physical injury" means physical pain, illness, or any impairment
29 of physical condition including, but not limited to, bruising, lacerations, hematomas, welts, or
30 permanent or temporary disfigurement and impairment of any bodily function or organ. The term
31 "serious physical injury" means a physical injury that creates a substantial risk of death or that
32 causes serious disfigurement or protracted loss or impairment of the function of any part of the
33 body.
34 2. Any appeal of a suspension imposed under subsection 1 of this section shall be a
35 direct appeal of the court order and subject to review by the presiding judge of the circuit court
36 or another judge within the circuit other than the judge who issued the original order to suspend
