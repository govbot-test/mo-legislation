HB 2780
Offered By

1 AMEND House Committee Substitute for House Bill Nos. 2780 & 2668, Page 40, Section
2 137.115, Line 124, by deleting all of said line and inserting in lieu thereof the following:
3 "9. For the tax year ending on or before December 31, 2026, the assessor of each county
4 and each city not within a county shall use a nationally"; and
5
6 Further amend said bill, page, and section, Line 142, by inserting after the number "10."
7 following:
8
9 "For all tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2027, the assessor of each county and
10 each city not within a county shall use the manufacturer's suggested retail price for all
11 manufactured motor vehicles as acquired annually by the state tax commission for the original
12 value in money of all motor vehicle assessment valuations. For the purposes of this subsection,
13 the term "original value in money" means the manufacturer's suggested retail price. For the
14 purposes of this subsection, the term "motor vehicles" means trucks, automobiles, motorcycles,
15 boats, trailers, and other motor vehicles required to be registered and titled pursuant to the
16 provisions of the motor vehicle and registration laws of this state. The following twelve-year
17 depreciation schedule shall be applied to each manufacturer's suggested retail price to develop
18 the annual and historical valuation guide for all motor vehicles. The values shall be delivered to
19 each software vendor not later than November fifteenth annually and vendors shall have the
20 values in place by December fifteenth annually for use in the next assessment year. In the
21 absence of a listing for a particular motor vehicle in such publication, the assessor shall use such
22 information or publications that, in the assessor's judgment, will fairly estimate the original value
23 in money of the motor vehicle and the assessor shall apply the appropriate depreciation from the
24 table as follows:
Year Percent Depreciation
Current 15
1 25
2 30.9
3 36.8

2 To implement the new schedule without large variations from the current method, the assessor

4 valuations shall be depreciated from the above table until the end of their useful life. The state
5 tax commission shall, with the assistance of the Missouri State Assessors' Association, develop
6 the bid specifications to select and secure the original manufacturer's suggested retail price from
7 a nationally recognized service. The state tax commission shall secure an annual appropriation
8 from the legislature for the guide and the programming necessary to allow valuation by vehicle
9 identification number in all certified mass appraisal software systems used in the state. The state
10 tax commission or the state of Missouri shall be the registered user of the value guide with rights
11 to allow all assessors access to the guide and to an online site. The state tax commission or state
12 shall be responsible for renewals and annual software cost for preparing the data in a usable
13 format for approved personal property software vendors in the state. If a county creates its own
14 software, it shall meet the same standards as the approved vendors. The data shall be available
15 to all vendors by November fifteenth annually. All vendors shall have the data available for use
16 in their client counties by December fifteenth prior to the January first assessment date. When
17 the manufacturer’s suggested retail price data is not available from the approved source or the
18 assessor deems it not appropriate for the vehicle value he or she is valuing, the assessor may
19 obtain a manufacturer’s suggested retail price from a source he or she deems reliable and apply
20 the depreciation schedule set out above.
