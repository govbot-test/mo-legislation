HOUSE BILL NO. 2303
103RD GENERAL ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY REPRESENTATIVE OEHLERKING.
5784H.01I JOSEPH ENGLER, Chief Clerk
AN ACT
To amend chapter 170, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to personal finance
curriculum in schools.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Chapter 170, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto one new section, to be
2 known as section 170.281, to read as follows:
170.281. 1. As used in this section, "personal finance" means a course consisting
2 of financial literacy and up-to-date tools, resources, and discipline necessary to succeed
3 in a personal and professional capacity in the current economy.
4 2. (1) The department of elementary and secondary education shall convene a
5 work group to develop and recommend academic performance standards relating to the
6 one-half unit of credit of personal finance required by the state board of education. The
7 work group shall include, but not be limited to:
8 (a) Educators providing instruction in personal finance;
9 (b) A representative from the department of elementary and secondary
10 education; and
11 (c) Up to two representatives from each of the following:
12 a. The banking industry;
13 b. Entrepreneurs in the business community;
14 c. Nonprofit organizations that focus on educating young professionals and
15 entrepreneurs;
16 d. The investment industry;
17 e. The student loan industry;

18 f. The retirement planning industry; and
19 g. The insurance industry.
20 (2) The total number of members of the work group shall be determined by the
21 department. At least twenty-five percent of the total shall be educators providing
22 instruction in personal finance.
23 3. The state board of education shall adopt and implement academic
24 performance standards relating to personal finance for the 2027-28 school year and
25 all subsequent school years, except that academic performance standards relating to
26 personal finance shall be reviewed every seven years to determine if the performance
27 standards need to be updated to reflect trends and best practices in the current
28 economy.
29 4. (1) For the 2027-28 school year and all subsequent school years, each school
30 district shall require that after the completion of grade nine each student satisfactorily
31 completes such one-half unit of credit of personal finance before receiving a high school
32 diploma or certificate of graduation.
33 (2) A school district may elect to waive the requirements of subdivision (1) of this
34 subsection for a student who transfers from outside the state to a Missouri high school if
35 the student can furnish documentation deemed acceptable by the school district of the
36 student's successful completion of a substantially similar course of instruction.
37 (3) A school district may allow a student in grade nine to complete such one-half
38 unit of credit of personal finance if, on the recommendation of a school counselor,
39 completing such one-half unit of credit of personal finance is beneficial and appropriate
40 for such student's personal plan of study or career academic plan.
41 5. The requirements of section 160.514 shall not apply to this section.
