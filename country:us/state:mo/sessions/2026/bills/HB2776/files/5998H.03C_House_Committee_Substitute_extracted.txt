AN ACT
To amend chapter 168, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to associate teacher
certificates.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Chapter 168, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto one new section, to be
2 known as section 168.038, to read as follows:
168.038. 1. In addition to granting certificates of license to teach in public
2 schools of the state under section 168.021, the state board of education shall grant
3 associate teacher certificates, as provided in this section, to any individual seeking to
4 teach in grades one through six in any public school in this state.
5 2. (1) The state board of education shall not grant an associate teacher
6 certificate under this section to any individual who has not completed a background
7 check as required under section 168.133.
8 (2) The state board of education may refuse to issue or renew, suspend, or revoke
9 any certificate sought or issued under this section in the same manner and for the same
10 reasons as under section 168.071.
11 3. (1) The state board of education shall grant a tier I associate teacher
12 certificate under this section to any individual who has submitted an application to the
13 state board of education after having completed either:
14 (a) A state-approved associate of applied science-level teacher preparation
15 program; or
16 (b) A teacher preparation program of a substantially similar nature as the
17 associate of applied science that includes at least sixty semester hours at an accredited
18 institution of higher education.

19 (2) The teacher preparation program shall:
20 (a) Include literacy and reading instruction coursework as outlined in subsection
21 4 of section 161.097;
22 (b) Be accredited by the state board of education in the same manner that
23 existing teacher preparation programs are accredited; and
24 (c) Include a capstone project as a component, which may include passage of the
25 Missouri general education assessment or a similar institution-created measure to assess
26 the individual's proficiency in fundamentals of instruction.
27 (3) A tier I associate teacher certificate issued by the state board of education
28 under this subsection shall certify the holder to be employed by a school district or
29 charter school as a teacher of record. Such certificate shall not be renewed and shall
30 expire four years after issuance.
31 (4) A holder of a tier I associate teacher certificate shall:
32 (a) Participate in a mentoring program approved and provided by the school
33 district or charter school for a minimum of two years; and
34 (b) Participate in school district or charter school professional development
35 aligned with the beginning teacher assistance program.
36 4. (1) In order to qualify for a tier II associate teacher certificate, an individual
37 who holds a tier I associate teacher certificate shall:
38 (a) Complete two consecutive years of service in a Missouri public school district
39 or charter school as a teacher of record;
40 (b) Successfully achieve an acceptable score on the state-approved teacher
41 evaluation system from:
42 a. Seven walk-through evaluations;
43 b. Two formative evaluations; and
44 c. One summative evaluation for each of the two probationary years such
45 individual held a tier I associate teacher certificate; and
46 (c) Be offered a third contract by a public school of the state.
47 (2) For an applicant who has a change in job status because of a reduction in the
48 workforce or a change in life circumstances, the consecutive years of service and score
49 required under this subsection may be achieved in any school district or charter school
50 during the four-year nonrenewable tier I associate teacher certificate period.
51 (3) A tier II associate teacher certificate issued by the state board of education
52 under this subsection shall certify the holder to be employed by a school district or
53 charter school for three years. Such certificate shall not be renewed and shall expire
54 three years after issuance.

55 5. A tier III associate teacher certificate may be issued to a holder of a tier II
56 associate teacher certificate who has completed three years of service and sixty contact
57 hours of professional development, which may include hours spent in an appropriate
58 college curriculum. Tier III associate teacher certificates shall expire after ninety-nine
59 years from issuance.
60 6. Holders of a tier I and tier II associate teacher certificate:
61 (1) Are not eligible for additional certifications under subdivision (7) of
62 subsection 1 of section 168.021;
63 (2) Shall be considered employees for the purposes of sections 169.600 to 169.715
64 and pay into the public education employee retirement system established under
65 sections 169.600 to 169.715; and
66 (3) Shall not be considered teachers for the purposes of sections 169.010 to
67 169.141.
68
69 School districts and charter schools shall be required to compensate tier I and II
70 associate teacher certificate holders at a minimum of thirty-five thousand dollars and
71 may provide a separate salary schedule for associate teacher certificate holders.
72 7. (1) A holder of a tier III associate teacher certificate shall be eligible for
73 additional certifications under subdivision (7) of subsection 1 of section 168.021. Such
74 additional certification shall be at the determination of the department of elementary
75 and secondary education, shall only include certifications for early childhood and at the
76 middle school level, and shall not include certification for special education or at the
77 high school level.
78 (2) A holder of a tier III associate teacher certificate shall be considered a
79 teacher for the purposes of sections 169.010 to 169.141 and pay into the public school
80 retirement system under sections 169.010 to 169.141.
81 (3) A holder of a tier III associate teacher certificate who is employed by a school
82 district or charter school shall be required to be compensated in accordance with the
83 minimum salary level established under section 163.172. Such compensation may be
84 included on a separate salary schedule for associate teacher certificate holders.
85 8. Mentors for tier I associate teacher certificate holders shall meet the
86 standards established under section 161.375 and shall be provided with additional
87 compensation which may include additional release time, and shall include sufficient
88 time for mentors to observe beginning teachers and for the beginning teachers to
89 observe master teachers, structured to provide multiple opportunities over time and to
90 minimize the need to require substitute teachers to facilitate observation.
