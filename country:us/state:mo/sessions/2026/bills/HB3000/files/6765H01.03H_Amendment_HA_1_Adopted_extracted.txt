HB 3000
Offered By

1 AMEND House Bill No. 3000, Page 3, Section 29.230, Line 60, by inserting after all of said
2 section and line the following:
3
4 "321.295. 1. The board of directors of a fire protection district may distribute surplus or
5 unneeded supplies or property to:
6 (1) volunteer fire protection associations, as defined in section 320.300, fire protection
7 districts, and fire departments;
8 (2) any organization registered as a nonprofit organization under 26 U.S.C. Section
9 501(c)3, as amended;
10 (3) public service corporations that provide training to fire departments, emergency
11 medical technicians, or police officers in search and recovery techniques, water rescue, ice
12 rescue, or watercraft operation; and
13 (4) other eligible donees, as such term is defined in connection with the federal surplus
14 property program;
15
16 in the same manner as provided for the distribution of federal surplus property.
17 2. The board of directors of a fire protection district may sell surplus or unneeded
18 supplies or property that are not transferred to fire protection associations, districts, or
19 departments or eligible donees to the general public. For items with a value of five hundred
20 dollars or less, the board shall obtain the name of the purchaser, their phone number and address,
21 and a description of the property sold, the amount paid, and the date. All items with a value over
22 five hundred dollars shall be sold on an online auction platform approved by the office of
23 administration or by sealed bid.
24 3. Notwithstanding the provisions of subsections 1 and 2 of this section to the contrary,
25 the board of directors of a fire protection district shall not donate or sell surplus emergency
26 vehicles or unneeded supplies or property to board members, officers, or employees of the fire
27 protection district or their relatives within the fourth degree by consanguinity or affinity, nor to
28 an entity of which a board member, officer, or employee is a board member or officer unless
29 such surplus is sold on an online auction platform approved by the Missouri office of
30 administration.
31 4. The board of directors of a fire protection district, or any person designated by the
32 board, may conduct or authorize an audit of any transfer, donation, or sale made under this
33 section. Such audit may include review of records, receipts, and documentation maintained
34 under this section to ensure compliance with applicable laws, regulations, and policies of the
35 district. The results of any audit conducted under this subsection shall be made available to the

1 governing body of the fire protection district and retained in accordance with applicable records
2 retention schedules."; and
3
4 Further amend said bill by amending the title, enacting clause, and intersectional references
5 accordingly.
