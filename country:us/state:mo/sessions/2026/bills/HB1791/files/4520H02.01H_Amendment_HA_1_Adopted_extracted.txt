4 321.228. 1. As used in this section, the following terms shall mean:
5 (1) "Residential construction", new construction and erection of detached single-family

7 two-family dwellings;
8 (2) "Residential construction regulatory system", any bylaw, ordinance, order, rule, or
9 regulation adopted, implemented, or enforced by any city, town, village, or county that pertains
10 to residential construction, to any permitting system, or program relating to residential
11 construction, including but not limited to the use or occupancy by the initial occupant thereof, or
12 to any system or program for the inspection of residential construction. Residential construction
13 regulatory system also includes the whole or any part of a nationally recognized model code,
14 with or without amendments specific to such city, town, village, or county.
15 2. Notwithstanding the provisions of any other law to the contrary, if a city, town,
16 village, or county adopts or has adopted, implements, and enforces a residential construction
17 regulatory system applicable to residential construction within its jurisdiction, any fire protection
18 districts wholly or partly located within such city, town, village, or county shall be without
19 power, authority, or privilege to enforce or implement a residential construction regulatory
20 system purporting to be applicable to any residential construction within such city, town, village,
21 or county. Notwithstanding the provisions of any other law to the contrary, any such residential
22 construction regulatory system adopted by a fire protection district or its board shall be treated as
23 advisory only and shall not be enforced by such fire protection district or its board.
24 3. Notwithstanding the provisions of any other law to the contrary, fire protection
25 districts:
26 (1) Shall have final regulatory authority regarding the location and specifications of fire
27 hydrants, fire hydrant flow rates, and fire lanes, all as it relates to residential construction.
28 Nothing in this subdivision shall be construed to require the political subdivision supplying
29 water to incur any costs to modify its water supply infrastructure; and
30 (2) May inspect the alteration, enlargement, replacement or repair of a detached single-
31 family or two-family dwelling; and
32 (3) Shall not collect a fee for the services described in subdivisions (1) and (2) of this
33 subsection.

38 purpose of serving residential construction.
