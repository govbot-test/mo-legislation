Section 1:
SECOND REGULAR SESSION
103RD GENERA L ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY SENATOR NURRENBERN.
3852S.01I KRISTINA MARTIN, Secretary
AN ACT
To repeal section 307.179, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof one new section relating to child
passenger restraint systems, with penalty provisions.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Section 307.179, RSMo, is repealed and one new
2 section enacted in lieu thereof, to be known as section 307.179,
3 to read as follows:
1 307.179. 1. As used in this section, the following
2 terms shall mean:
3 (1) "Child booster seat", a seating system which meets
4 the Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards set forth in 49
5 C.F.R. 571.213, as amended, that is designed to elevate a
6 child to properly sit in a federally approved safety belt
7 system;
8 (2) "Child passenger restraint system", a seating
9 system which meets the Federal Motor Vehicle Safety
10 Standards set forth in 49 C.F.R. 571.213, as amended, and
11 which is either permanently affixed to a motor vehicle or is
12 affixed to such vehicle by a safety belt or a universal
13 attachment system;
14 (3) "Driver", a person who is in actual physical
15 control of a motor vehicle.
16 2. Every driver transporting a child under the age of
17 sixteen years shall be responsible, when transporting such

and is intended to be omitted in the law.

19 streets or highways of this state, for providing for the

21 (1) Children shall be secured in a rear-facing child
22 passenger restraint system until the child reaches two years
23 of age, except that a child under two years of age may be
24 secured in a forward-facing system when the child reaches
25 the highest rear-facing weight or height allowed by the

56 motor vehicle is designed only for a front seat area. The
57 driver transporting children referred to in this subsection
58 is not in violation of this section].

60 passenger restraint system or vehicle safety belt for
61 children less than sixteen years of age being transported in
62 a motor vehicle.
63 3. Any driver who violates [subdivision (1), (2), or
64 (3) of subsection 2 of] this section is guilty of an
65 infraction and, upon conviction, may be punished by a fine
66 of not more than fifty dollars and court costs. [Any driver
67 who violates subdivision (4) of subsection 2 of this section
68 shall be subject to the penalty in subsection 6 of section
69 307.178. If a driver receives a citation for violating
70 subdivision (1), (2), or (3) of subsection 2 of this
71 section, the charges shall be dismissed or withdrawn if the
72 driver prior to or at his or her hearing provides evidence
73 of acquisition of a child passenger restraint system or
74 child booster seat which is satisfactory to the court or the
75 party responsible for prosecuting the driver's citation.]
76 4. The provisions of this section shall not apply to
77 any public carrier for hire. The provisions of this section
78 shall not apply to students four years of age or older who
79 are passengers on a school bus designed for carrying eleven
80 passengers or more and which is manufactured or equipped

82 school buses are defined in section 301.010.
83 5. The highways and transportation commission shall
84 initiate and develop a program of public information to
85 develop understanding of, and ensure compliance with, the
86 provisions of this section.
