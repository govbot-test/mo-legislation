1 AMEND House Bill No. 2498, Page 9, Section 211.151, Line 38, by inserting after said section
2 and line the following:
3
4 "211.261. 1. An appeal shall be allowed to the child from any final judgment, order or
5 decree made under the provisions of this chapter and may be taken on the part of the child by its
6 parent, guardian, legal custodian, spouse, relative or next friend. An appeal shall be allowed to a
7 parent from any final judgment, order or decree made under the provisions of this chapter which
8 adversely affects him. An appeal shall be allowed to the juvenile officer, prosecuting attorney,
9 or attorney general from any final judgment, order or decree made under this chapter, except that
10 no such appeal shall be allowed concerning a final determination pursuant to subdivision (3) of
11 subsection 1 of section 211.031. Notice of appeal shall be filed within thirty days after the final
12 judgment, order or decree has been entered but neither the notice of appeal nor any motion filed
13 subsequent to the final judgment acts as a supersedeas unless the court so orders.
14 2. Notwithstanding the provisions of subsection 1 of this section, an appeal shall be
15 allowed to the:
16 (1) Juvenile officer from any order suppressing evidence, a confession or an admission,
17 in proceedings under subdivision (3) of subsection 1 of section 211.031;
18 (2) Juvenile officer, prosecuting attorney, or attorney general under section 211.071; or
19 [(2)] (3) Parent, guardian ad litem, child's counsel, or juvenile officer from any order
20 changing or modifying the placement of a child.
21 3. The appeal provided for in subsection 2 of this section shall be an interlocutory
22 appeal, filed in the appropriate district of the Missouri court of appeals. Notice of such
23 interlocutory appeal shall be filed within three days of the entry of the order of trial court; the
24 time limits applicable to such appeal shall be the same as in interlocutory appeals allowed to the
25 state in criminal cases."; and

27 Further amend said bill by amending the title, enacting clause, and intersectional references
28 accordingly.
