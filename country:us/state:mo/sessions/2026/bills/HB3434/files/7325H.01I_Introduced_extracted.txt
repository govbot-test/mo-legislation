AN ACT
To amend chapter 595, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to the
establishment of the Missouri statewide sexual assault response task force.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Chapter 595, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto one new section, to be
2 known as section 595.410, to read as follows:
595.410. 1. There is hereby established the "Missouri Statewide Sexual Assault
2 Response Task Force", which shall be composed of the following members:
3 (1) The following four members of the general assembly:
4 (a) Two members of the senate, with one member to be appointed by the
5 president pro tempore of the senate and one member to be appointed by the minority
6 floor leader of the senate; and
7 (b) Two members of the house of representatives, with one member to be
8 appointed by the speaker of the house of representatives and one member to be
9 appointed by the minority floor leader of the house of representatives;
10 (2) Three representatives of the attorney general's office, with one member
11 representing the cold case section of the litigation division, one member representing the
12 SAFE Kits Initiative, and one member representing SAFETrack, with each such
13 member appointed by the attorney general or his or her designee;
14 (3) One representative of the department of public safety's crime victims'
15 compensation staff, to be appointed by the director of the department of public safety or
16 his or her designee;

40 2. The task force shall study best practices and make recommendations
41 regarding the statewide response to sexual assault from the time of the complaint
42 through each phase of the investigative, medical, advocacy, and justice system response.
43 3. The task force shall determine whether a need exists for additional employees
44 or volunteers of a rape crisis center for victims of sexual assault and, if such a need does
45 exist:
46 (1) Establish a plan for how the state can provide, in conjunction with rape crisis
47 centers, victims' advocate organizations, the department of social services, and the
48 department of public safety, additional employees or volunteers of a rape crisis center to
49 meet the needs identified; and
50 (2) Determine the cost of funding such a plan.
51 4. The task force shall evaluate whether a need exists to provide for ongoing
52 evaluation of the implementation of the Sexual Assault Survivor Bill of Rights under
53 section 595.201, and if it is determined that such a need exists, the task force shall:

54 (1) Identify the scope and nature of the need; and
55 (2) Make recommendations on how best to fill that need, whether legislatively or
56 otherwise.
57 5. The task force shall:
58 (1) Collect data regarding sexual assault reporting, arrests, prosecution rates,
59 access to sexual assault victims services, and any other data important for its
60 deliberations and recommendations;
61 (2) Collect feedback from stakeholders, practitioners, and leadership throughout
62 the state and local law enforcement, victim services, forensic science practitioners, and
63 health care communities to inform development of future best practices or clinical
64 guidelines regarding the care and treatment of survivors;
65 (3) Identify gaps and barriers in response to sexual assault victims and propose
66 corrective measures; and
67 (4) Identify barriers to successful prosecution of sexual assault crimes and
68 propose remedies.
69 6. The office of the attorney general shall provide administrative support to the
70 task force.
71 7. On or before December 31, 2028, the task force shall submit a report on its
72 findings to the governor and general assembly. The report shall include any dissenting
73 opinions in addition to any majority opinions.
74 8. The task force shall expire on December 31, 2028.
