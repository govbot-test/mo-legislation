AN ACT
To repeal section 650.475, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof three new sections relating to
immigration.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Section 650.475, RSMo, is repealed and three new sections enacted in lieu
2 thereof, to be known as sections 1.3050, 590.656, and 650.475, to read as follows:
1.3050. No state funds, including in the form of personnel or facilities, shall be
2 used in the enforcement of discriminatory immigration policies, including policies based
3 on racial profiling or that violate the constitutional rights of individuals.
590.656. All peace officers of the state or any political subdivision thereof shall
2 participate in training on racial profiling and constitutional rights, including
3 constitutional rights of immigrants. Such training shall occur at least yearly.
650.475. 1. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, no government entity,
2 political subdivision, or government official within the state of Missouri shall prohibit, or in
3 any way restrict, any government entity or official from communicating or cooperating with
4 the United States Bureau of Immigration and Customs Enforcement regarding the citizenship
5 or immigration status, lawful or unlawful, of any individual. The provisions of this
6 subsection shall apply only when there is a clear and documented public safety concern.
7 2. Municipalities and political subdivisions may collect and share the identity of
8 persons by the same means the Federal Bureau of Investigation or its successor agency uses in
9 its Integrated Automated Fingerprint Identification System or its successor program.
10 3. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, no person or agency within the state of
11 Missouri shall prohibit, or in any way restrict, a public employee from doing any of the
