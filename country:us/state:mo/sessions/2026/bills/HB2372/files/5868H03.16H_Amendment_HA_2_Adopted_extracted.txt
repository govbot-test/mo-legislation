4 "191.455. 1. Sections 191.455 to 191.467 shall be known and may be cited as the "Hope

6 2. As used in sections 191.455 to 191.467, unless the context otherwise requires, the
7 following terms mean:
8 (1) "Eligible facility", an institution that is operating under a Federalwide Assurance
9 (FWA) for the Protection of Human Subjects under 42 U.S.C. Section 289(a) and 45 CFR Part
10 46 and that is subject to the FWA laws, regulations, policies, and guidelines, including renewals
11 or updates;
12 (2) "Eligible patient", an individual who meets the following conditions:
13 (a) Has considered all other treatment options currently approved by the United States
14 Food and Drug Administration;
15 (b) Has received a recommendation from his or her physician for an individualized
16 investigational treatment based on analysis of the patient's genomic sequence; human
17 chromosomes; deoxyribonucleic acid; ribonucleic acid; genes; gene products, such as enzymes
18 and other types of proteins; or metabolites;
19 (c) Has a life-threatening or severely debilitating illness, or a serious disease or condition
20 associated with morbidity that has a substantial impact on day-to-day functioning, as attested to
21 by the patient's treating physician;
22 (d) Has given written, informed consent for the use of the individualized investigational
23 drug, biological product, or device; and
24 (e) Has documentation from his or her physician that he or she meets the requirements of
25 paragraphs (a) to (d) of this subdivision;
26 (3) "Individualized investigational drug, biological product, or device", any drug,
27 biological product, or device that is unique to and produced exclusively for use for an individual
28 patient based on the patient's own genetic profile. The term "individualized investigational drug,
29 biological product, or device":

6 191.457. 1. A manufacturer operating within an eligible facility and in accordance with
7 all applicable Federalwide Assurance laws and regulations may make available an individualized

11 191.455 to 191.467 shall not require that a manufacturer make available an individualized
12 investigational drug, biological product, or device to an eligible patient.
13 2. An eligible facility or manufacturer operating within an eligible facility may do all of
14 the following:
15 (1) Provide an individualized investigational drug, biological product, or device to an

18 manufacture of the individualized investigational drug, biological product, or device.
19 191.459. 1. Sections 191.455 to 191.467 shall not expand the coverage required of an
20 insurer under chapter 376.
21 2. A health plan, third-party administrator, or governmental agency may, but is not
22 required to, provide coverage for the cost of an individualized investigational drug, biological
23 product, or device or the cost of services related to the use of an individualized investigational
24 drug, biological product, or device in accordance with sections 191.455 to 191.467.
25 3. Sections 191.455 to 191.467 shall not require any governmental agency to pay costs
26 associated with the use of or care or treatment of a patient with an individualized investigational
