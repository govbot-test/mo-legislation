14 2. As used in this section, the following terms mean:
15 (1) "Medically tailored groceries", a selection of groceries, including but not limited to
16 fruits, vegetables, grains, beans, lean proteins, or dairy, that is prescribed by a registered dietitian
17 or qualified clinical team for individuals who are able to prepare food. Such groceries may be
18 specifically selected by a registered dietitian or qualified clinical team, or selected by a
19 participant from a pantry in accordance with dietary recommendations set by a registered
20 dietitian or qualified clinical team;
21 (2) "Medically tailored meals", meals used to meet the specific dietary needs of
22 individuals living with one or more chronic conditions or diet-related conditions. Such meals are
23 prescribed by a registered dietitian or qualified clinical team and home-delivered for individuals
24 who are not able to prepare food on their own;
25 (3) "Produce prescriptions", the prescription of fruits, vegetables, or other healthy foods
26 with no added fats, sugars, or salts to at-risk patients by a registered dietitian or qualified clinical
27 team, typically in the form of coupons or vouchers for local farmers' markets, grocery stores, or
28 mobile markets.
29 3. The department of social services shall apply to the Centers for Medicare and
30 Medicaid Services of the federal Department of Health and Human Services for a Section 1115
31 demonstration waiver to implement the "Food is Medicine" program for the purpose of providing
