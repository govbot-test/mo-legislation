4 "208.270. 1. This section shall be known and may be cited as the "Food is Medicine
5 Act".
6 2. As used in this section, the following terms mean:
7 (1) "Medically tailored groceries", a selection of groceries, including but not limited to
8 fruits, vegetables, grains, beans, lean proteins, or dairy, that is prescribed by a registered dietitian
9 or qualified clinical team for individuals who are able to prepare food;
10 (2) "Medically tailored meals", meals used to meet the specific dietary needs of
11 individuals living with one or more chronic conditions or diet-related conditions. Such meals are
12 prescribed by a registered dietitian or qualified clinical team and home-delivered for individuals
13 who are not able to prepare food on their own;
14 (3) "Produce prescriptions", the prescription of fruits, vegetables, or other healthy foods
15 with no added fats, sugars, or salts to at-risk patients by a registered dietitian or qualified clinical
16 team, typically in the form of coupons or vouchers for local farmers' markets, grocery stores, or
17 mobile markets.
18 3. The department of social services shall apply to the Centers for Medicare and
19 Medicaid Services of the federal Department of Health and Human Services for a Section 1115
20 demonstration waiver to implement the "Food is Medicine" program for the purpose of providing
21 nutritional support through the MO HealthNet program. The food is medicine program shall be
22 designed to improve health outcomes for MO HealthNet participants with nutrition-related
23 chronic diseases through nutrition services and to reduce the need for medical care for those
24 participants.
25 4. Nutrition services covered under this program may include, but shall not be limited to,
26 the following:
27 (1) Case management;
28 (2) Nutrition counseling; and
29 (3) Food provisions prescribed by a registered dietitian or qualified clinical team,
30 including:
