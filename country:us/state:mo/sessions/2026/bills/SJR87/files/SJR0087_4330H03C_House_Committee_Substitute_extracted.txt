Section 3:
Section 15. 1. Except as provided in subsection 2 of this section, each county
2 shall elect a sheriff for a term of four years by a majority of the qualified voters of the
3 county voting thereon at the time of voting designated for such office provided by law as
4 of the effective date of this section and every four years thereafter.
5 2. The provisions of this section shall not apply to:
6 (1) Any city not within a county;

9 (3) Any county with a charter form of government which has a population
10 greater than four hundred thousand inhabitants and that is adjacent to a county with a
11 population greater than nine hundred thousand inhabitants.
12 3. Except as provided in subsection 2 of this section, the elected sheriff shall be
13 the chief law enforcement officer in his or her county. Notwithstanding Article VI,
14 Section 18(a) or any other article of the Missouri Constitution to the contrary, every
15 sheriff, except as provided in subsection 2 of this section, in addition to duties as set in
16 law, shall quell and suppress assaults, batteries, riots, routs, affrays, and insurrections
17 and shall:
18 (1) Apprehend and commit to jail all felons and traitors;
19 (2) Attend each division of the circuit court presided over by a circuit or
20 associate circuit court judge held in his or her county, when so directed by the court;
21 and
22 (3) Execute process, including writs of replevin, attachments, and final process
23 issued by circuit and associate circuit court judges, unless the sheriff of the county does
24 not perform such duties as of August 28, 2026.
25 4. Except as provided in subsection 2 of this section, every sheriff is a
26 conservator of the peace within his or her county and shall cause all offenders against
27 law to appear at the next term of the circuit or associate court of the county, and to be
28 committed to jail in case of failure to give the recognizance.
29 5. Except as provided in subsection 2 of this section, the sheriff shall hire and
30 appoint such deputies, assistants, and other employees as the sheriff deems necessary for
31 the proper discharge of the duties of his or her office, who shall hold office at the will
32 and pleasure of the sheriff and may set their compensation within the limits of the
33 allocations made for that purpose.
34 6. Except as provided in subsection 2 of this section, an elected county sheriff
35 shall not be removed from office except by writ of quo warranto initiated by the
36 attorney general.

Section 4:
Section B. Pursuant to chapter 116, and other applicable constitutional provisions and
2 laws of this state allowing the general assembly to adopt ballot language for the submission of
3 this joint resolution to the voters of this state, the official summary statement of this
4 resolution shall be as follows:
5 "Shall the Missouri Constitution be amended to support law enforcement by
6 preserving the right of citizens to elect a county sheriff, prohibiting the removal of a

7 county sheriff except by a writ of quo warranto, and recognizing the office of sheriff
8 as part of the administration of justice?".
