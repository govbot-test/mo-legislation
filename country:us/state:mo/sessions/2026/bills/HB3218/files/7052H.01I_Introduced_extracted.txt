AN ACT
To repeal sections 115.243, 115.449, 115.461, 115.511, and 128.010, RSMo, and to enact in
lieu thereof six new sections relating to the election of presidential electors.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Sections 115.243, 115.449, 115.461, 115.511, and 128.010, RSMo, are
2 repealed and six new sections enacted in lieu thereof, to be known as sections 115.243,
3 115.449, 115.461, 115.511, 128.010, and 128.075, to read as follows:
115.243. 1. For the purposes of this chapter, the candidates for president and vice
2 president of the United States from any political party or group of petitioners shall be
3 considered one candidate. The names of the candidates for president and vice president from
4 each political party or group of petitioners shall be enclosed in a brace directly to the left of
5 the names in the appropriate column on the official ballot. Directly to the left of each brace
6 shall be printed one square, the sides of which are not less than one-fourth inch in length. The
7 names of candidates for presidential electors shall not be printed on the ballot but shall be
8 filed with the secretary of state in the manner provided in section 115.399.
9 2. A vote for any candidate for president and vice president on the official ballot
10 shall be a vote for [their electors] each of the candidates for elector filed under section
11 115.399.
12 3. When presidential and vice-presidential candidates are to be elected, the following
13 instruction shall be printed on the official ballot: "A vote for candidates for President and
14 Vice President is a vote for [their electors] each of the candidates for elector.".

115.511. 1. The secretary of state shall convene the board of state canvassers to total

3 receiving all required abstracts from the primary election, issue a statement announcing the
4 results of the primary election for federal officers, governor, lieutenant governor, state
5 senators and representatives, circuit judges, secretary of state, attorney general, state treasurer
6 and state auditor.

23 4. In issuing a statement announcing the results of an election for elector of
24 president and vice president of the United States, the board shall announce each
25 candidate for elector as receiving a number of votes equal to those cast on the official
26 ballot for the elector's associated candidates for president and vice president as filed

28 president as receiving a vote total equal to the aggregate votes of all candidates for
29 elector associated with president and vice president as filed under section 115.399. No
30 other vote total shall be announced by the board for any candidate for president and
31 vice president.
128.010. Hereafter, at all elections held in this state for the offices of president and
2 vice president of the United States and for elector of president and vice president, the
3 electoral district shall be the same as the congressional districts into which the state shall be
4 divided, and the same number of electors as there may be districts shall be chosen by the
5 qualified voters, one of whom shall be a resident of each district; and in addition thereto, two
6 electors shall be chosen [by the state at large] irrespective of district residence.
128.075. As soon as practicable and no later than six days before the date of the
2 meeting of electors, the governor shall send a certificate of ascertainment of
3 appointment of electors to the Archivist of the United States. The certificate shall
4 state the names of the electors and the number of votes each candidate for elector
5 received under section 115.243. The governor shall aggregate the votes received by each
6 candidate for elector with each other candidate for elector nominated by the same party
