Section 1:
SECOND REGULAR SESSION
103RD GENERA L ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY SENATOR BROWN (16).
7452S.01I KRISTINA MARTIN, Secretary
AN ACT
To repeal sections 313.820 and 313.822, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof two new sections
relating to excursion gambling boats.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Sections 313.820 and 313.822, RSMo, are
2 repealed and two new sections enacted in lieu thereof, to be
3 known as sections 313.820 and 313.822, to read as follows:
1 313.820. 1. (1) An excursion boat licensee shall pay
2 to the commission an admission fee of [two] five dollars for
3 each person embarking on an excursion gambling boat with a
4 ticket of admission. One dollar of such fee shall be
5 deposited to the credit of the gaming commission fund as
6 authorized pursuant to section 313.835, and [one dollar]
7 four dollars of such fee shall not be considered state funds
8 and shall be paid [to the home dock city or county] as
9 follows:
10 (a) One dollar of such fee shall be paid to the home
11 dock city or county;
12 (b) One dollar of such fee shall be remitted to the
13 Missouri department of mental health developmental
14 disabilities division for payments solely for providing
15 community services, ongoing learning, education, and support
16 to people with developmental disabilities, including, but
17 not limited to, the purchase of services from providers
EXPLANATION-Matter enclosed in bold-faced brackets [thus] in this bill is not enacted
and is intended to be omitted in the law.
SB 1723 2
18 designated by the division of developmental disabilities,
19 but not for the support of the facilities operated by the
20 department of mental health; and
21 (c) Two dollars of such fee shall be paid to the
22 veterans commission capital improvement trust fund.
23 (2) Subject to appropriation, one cent of such fee
24 deposited to the credit of the gaming commission fund may be
25 deposited to the credit of the compulsive gamblers fund
26 created pursuant to the provisions of section 313.842.
27 Nothing in this section shall preclude any licensee from
28 charging any amount deemed necessary for a ticket of
29 admission to any person embarking on an excursion gambling
30 boat. If tickets are issued which are good for more than
31 one excursion, the admission fee shall be paid to the
32 commission for each person using the ticket on each
33 excursion that the ticket is used. If free passes or
34 complimentary admission tickets are issued, the excursion
35 boat licensee shall pay to the commission the same fee upon
36 these passes or complimentary tickets as if they were sold
37 at the regular and usual admission rate; however, the
38 excursion boat licensee may issue fee-free passes to actual
39 and necessary officials and employees of the licensee or
40 other persons actually working on the excursion gambling
41 boat. The issuance of fee-free passes is subject to the
42 rules of the commission, and a list of all persons to whom
43 the fee-free passes are issued shall be filed with the
44 commission.
45 2. All licensees are subject to all income taxes,
46 sales taxes, earnings taxes, use taxes, property taxes or
47 any other tax or fee now or hereafter lawfully levied by any
48 political subdivision; however, no other license tax, permit
49 tax, occupation tax, excursion fee, or taxes or fees shall
SB 1723 3
50 be imposed, levied or assessed exclusively upon licensees by
51 a political subdivision. All state taxes not connected
52 directly to gambling games shall be collected by the
53 department of revenue. Notwithstanding the provisions of
54 section 32.057 to the contrary, the department of revenue
55 may furnish and the commission may receive tax information
56 to determine if applicants or licensees are complying with
57 the tax laws of this state; however, any tax information
58 acquired by the commission shall not become public record
59 and shall be used exclusively for commission business.
1 313.822. A tax is imposed on the adjusted gross
2 receipts received from gambling games authorized pursuant to
3 sections 313.800 to 313.850 at the rate of [twenty-one]
4 thirty-four percent. The taxes imposed by this section
5 shall be returned to the commission in accordance with the
6 commission's rules and regulations who shall transfer such
7 taxes to the director of revenue. All checks and drafts
8 remitted for payment of these taxes and fees shall be made
9 payable to the director of revenue. If the commission is
10 not satisfied with the return or payment made by any
11 licensee, it is hereby authorized and empowered to make an
12 assessment of the amount due based upon any information
13 within its possession or that shall come into its
14 possession. Any licensee against whom an assessment is made
15 by the commission may petition for a reassessment. The
16 request for reassessment shall be made within twenty days
17 from the date the assessment was mailed or delivered to the
18 licensee, whichever is earlier. Whereupon the commission
19 shall give notice of a hearing for reassessment and fix the
20 date upon which the hearing shall be held. The assessment
21 shall become final if a request for reassessment is not
22 received by the commission within the twenty days. Except
SB 1723 4
23 as provided in this section, on and after April 29, 1993,
24 all functions incident to the administration, collection,
25 enforcement, and operation of the tax imposed by sections
26 144.010 to 144.525 shall be applicable to the taxes and fees
27 imposed by this section.
28 (1) Each excursion gambling boat shall designate a
29 city or county as its home dock. The home dock city or
30 county may enter into agreements with an excursion gambling
31 boat and other cities or counties authorized pursuant to
32 subsection 10 of section 313.812 to share revenue obtained
33 [pursuant to this section] from the excursion gambling
34 boats' portion of adjusted gross receipts. The home dock
35 city or county shall receive ten percent of the first twenty-
36 one percent of the adjusted gross receipts tax collections,
37 as levied pursuant to this section, for use in providing
38 services necessary for the safety of the public visiting an
39 excursion gambling boat. Such home dock city or county
40 shall annually submit to the commission a shared revenue
41 agreement with any excursion gambling boat and any other
42 city or county. All moneys owed the home dock city or
43 county shall be deposited and distributed to such city or
44 county in accordance with rules and regulations of the
45 commission. All revenues provided for in this section to be
46 transferred to the governing body of any city not within a
47 county and any city with a population of over three hundred
48 fifty thousand inhabitants shall not be considered state
49 funds and shall be deposited in such city's general revenue
50 fund to be expended as provided for in this section.
51 (2) The remaining amount of the adjusted gross
52 receipts tax shall be deposited in the state treasury to the
53 credit of the "Gaming Proceeds for Education Fund" which is
54 hereby created in the state treasury. Moneys deposited in
SB 1723 5
55 this fund shall be kept separate from the general revenue
56 fund as well as any other funds or accounts in the state
57 treasury, shall be used solely for education pursuant to the
58 Missouri Constitution and shall be considered the proceeds
59 of excursion boat gambling and state funds pursuant to
60 Article IV, Section 15 of the Missouri Constitution. All
61 interest received on the gaming proceeds for education fund
62 shall be credited to the gaming proceeds for education
63 fund. Appropriation of the moneys deposited into the gaming
64 proceeds for education fund shall be pursuant to state law.
65 (3) The state auditor shall perform an annual audit of
66 the gaming proceeds for education fund, which shall include
67 the evaluation of whether appropriations for elementary and
68 secondary education have increased and are being used as
69 intended. The state auditor shall make copies of each audit
70 available to the public and to the general assembly.
