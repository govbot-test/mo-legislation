Section 1:
SECOND REGULAR SESSION
103RD GENERA L ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY SENATOR NICOLA.
5351S.02I KRISTINA MARTIN, Secretary
AN ACT
To repeal section 290.502, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof one new section relating to the
minimum wage rate.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Section 290.502, RSMo, is repealed and one new
2 section enacted in lieu thereof, to be known as section 290.502,
3 to read as follows:
1 290.502. 1. Except as may be otherwise provided
2 pursuant to sections 290.500 to 290.530, effective January
3 1, 2007, every employer shall pay to each employee wages at
4 the rate of $6.50 per hour, or wages at the same rate or
5 rates set under the provisions of federal law as the
6 prevailing federal minimum wage applicable to those covered
7 jobs in interstate commerce, whichever rate per hour is
8 higher.
9 2. Notwithstanding any provision of this section to
10 the contrary, any employer may pay any minor employee wages
11 at the rate of not less than $12.30 per hour, or wages at
12 the same rate or rates set under the provisions of federal
13 law as the prevailing federal minimum wage applicable to
14 those covered jobs in interstate commerce, whichever rate
15 per hour is higher. For purposes of this subsection, the
16 term "minor employee" means any employee who is less than
17 eighteen years of age.
EXPLANATION-Matter enclosed in bold-faced brackets [thus] in this bill is not enacted
and is intended to be omitted in the law.

32 to sections 290.500 to 290.530, and notwithstanding
33 subsection 1 of this section, effective January 1, 2025,
34 every employer shall pay to each employee wages at the rate
35 of not less than $13.75 per hour, or wages at the same rate
36 or rates set under the provisions of federal law as the
37 federal minimum wage applicable to those covered jobs in
38 interstate commerce, whichever rate per hour is higher.
39 Thereafter, the minimum wage established by this subsection
40 shall be increased by $1.25 per hour, to $15.00 per hour,
41 effective January 1, 2026. If at any time the federal
42 minimum wage rate is above or is thereafter increased above
43 the minimum wage then in effect under this subsection, the
44 higher federal rate shall immediately become the minimum
45 wage required by this subsection.
46 [4.] 5. For purposes of this section, the term "public

48 subdivision of the state, including a department, agency,
