Section 1:
SECOND REGULAR SESSION
103RD GENERAL ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY REPRESENTATIVE WELLENKAMP.
6735H.01I JOSEPH ENGLER, Chief Clerk
AN ACT
To repeal section 442.055, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof one new section relating to
contamination of radioactive material or other hazardous material at a premises, with a
penalty provision.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:

Section 2:
Section A. Section 442.055, RSMo, is repealed and one new section enacted in lieu
2 thereof, to be known as section 442.055, to read as follows:

Section 3:
442.055. 1. In the event that any premises currently rented, leased, sold,
2 transferred, or conveyed or any premises to be rented, leased, sold, transferred, or
3 conveyed is or was previously contaminated with radioactive material or other hazardous
4 material, the owner, seller, landlord, or other transferor shall disclose in writing to the
5 prospective lessee, current lessee, purchaser, or transferee the fact the premises is or was
6 previously contaminated with radioactive material or other hazardous material; provided that,
7 the owner, seller, landlord, or other transferor has empirically supported knowledge of such
8 radioactive or other hazardous contamination. In the event that an owner, seller, landlord, or
9 other transferor does not make the disclosure as required under this section, and the person
10 had knowledge of such radioactive or other hazardous contamination, the person shall be
11 guilty of a class A misdemeanor. As used in this section, the term "knowledge" shall require
12 the receipt by the owner, seller, landlord, or other transferor of a [report stating affirmatively]
13 written or electronic communication from a governmental agency that the premises is or
14 was previously contaminated with radioactive material or other hazardous material. If any
15 lessee learns that the owner, seller, landlord, or other transferor had knowledge and
16 failed to disclose such knowledge to the lessee, the lessee has the right to terminate his or
