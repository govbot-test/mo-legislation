SECOND REGULAR SESSION
SENATE COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE FOR
SENATE BILLS NOS. 977 & 1011
103RD GENERA L ASSEMBLY
5434S.02C KRISTINA MARTIN, Secretary
AN ACT
To amend chapters 1, 506, and 508, RSMo, by adding thereto three new sections relating to
application of foreign law.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Chapters 1, 506, and 508, RSMo, are amended by
2 adding thereto three new sections, to be known as sections
3 1.037, 506.602, and 508.074, to read as follows:
1 1.037. The World Health Organization, the United
2 Nations, the World Economic Forum, and any other
3 international organization or body shall have no
4 jurisdiction or power within the state of Missouri. No
5 rule, regulation, fee, tax, policy, or mandate of any kind
6 of the World Health Organization, the United Nations, the
7 World Economic Forum, or any other international
8 organization or body shall be enforced or implemented by the
9 state of Missouri or any agency, department, board, or
10 commission of the state or any municipality or other
11 political subdivision of the state.
1 506.602. 1. This section shall be known and may be
2 cited as the "No Shari'a Act".
3 2. As used in this section, the following terms mean:
4 (1) "Foreign law", any law, legal framework, legal
5 code, or system, including religious law when invoked as a
6 substitute for a state or federal law, derived from a

SCS SBs 977 & 1011 2
7 jurisdiction outside of any state or territory of the United
8 States, including international organizations and tribunals;
9 (2) "Fundamental rights", rights guaranteed by the
10 Constitution of Missouri and the Constitution of the United
11 States, including, but not limited to, due process, equal
12 protection, freedom of religion, freedom of speech, and
13 rights related to marriage, child custody, and property.
14 3. The application of Shari'a law or any foreign law
15 that denies the parties fundamental rights shall be
16 prohibited and render the following void and unenforceable:
17 (1) A ruling or decision of any state court,
18 arbitration panel, or administrative agency;
19 (2) A contract or contractual provision, if severable,
20 that provides for the choice of Shari'a law or any foreign
21 law; and
22 (3) A contract or contractual provision, if severable,
23 that grants jurisdiction to a foreign tribunal.
24 4. No court shall enforce or apply:
25 (1) A judgment, decree, or arbitration decision if it
26 relies, in whole or in part, on Shari'a law or any foreign
27 law that violates the fundamental rights of a party;
28 (2) A contract or contractual provision, if severable,
29 that provides for the choice of Shari'a law or any foreign
30 law where the enforcement or application would be in
31 violation of a party's fundamental rights; or
32 (3) Shari'a law or foreign law in matters involving
33 marriage, divorce, child custody, adoption, or inheritance
34 if such enforcement or application would be inconsistent
35 with fundamental rights or public policy.
36 5. The provisions of this section shall not be
37 construed to:

SCS SBs 977 & 1011 3
38 (1) Disapprove or abrogate existing precedent of the
39 Missouri supreme court;
40 (2) Limit adjudication of ecclesiastical matters of a
41 religious organization, including the selection,
42 appointment, discipline, or removal of clergy or
43 interpretation of doctrine;
44 (3) Apply to corporations, partnerships, or
45 associations that voluntarily subject themselves to any
46 foreign law or foreign courts; or
47 (4) Apply where federal law preempts state law,
48 including obligations under treaties or international
49 agreements.
1 508.074. No state court, arbitration panel, tribunal,
2 or administrative agency shall transfer any civil action if
3 such transfer would result in the application of Shari'a law
4 or any other foreign law, as the term "foreign law" is
5 defined in section 506.602, that would violate or likely
6 violate fundamental rights, as the term "fundamental rights"
7 is defined in section 506.602.
