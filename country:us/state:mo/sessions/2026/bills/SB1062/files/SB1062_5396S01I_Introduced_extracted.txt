Section 1:
SECOND REGULAR SESSION
103RD GENERA L ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY SENATOR CARTER.
5396S.01I KRISTINA MARTIN, Secretary
AN ACT
To amend chapter 37, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to the office of
administration.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Chapter 37, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto
2 one new section, to be known as section 37.840, to read as
3 follows:
1 37.840. 1. This section shall be known and may be
2 cited as the "Hope Missouri" act.
3 2. For purposes of this section, the following terms
4 mean:
5 (1) "Care plan", a written document that contains
6 information provided by the office of administration to its
7 participants for whom actionable steps and corresponding
8 timelines have been developed and customized to assist the
9 participants in achieving their immediate, intermediate, and
10 long-term goals. The care plan shall also address barriers
11 by directly connecting participants to community resources
12 and opportunities;
13 (2) "Hope Missouri partner network", a group of
14 entities, including state departments and agencies, public-
15 private partnerships, and nonprofit, private-sector, and
16 faith-based organizations, which is actively engaged in the
17 mission of the hope Missouri office and which provides goods
SB 1062 2
18 or services to directly support the participants' goals and
19 address identified barriers;
20 (3) "Hope line", a statewide toll-free telephone
21 number that serves as a hotline for hope Missouri inquiries
22 and referrals for services;
23 (4) "Hope navigators", individuals who coordinate care
24 and assist participants and families seeking services
25 through hope Missouri;
26 (5) "Office", the hope Missouri office within the
27 office of administration;
28 (6) "Participant", an individual who voluntarily
29 participates in hope Missouri;
30 (7) "Participating state agency", a state department,
31 division, or agency that takes part in hope Missouri at the
32 direction of the commissioner of administration.
33 3. (1) The "Hope Missouri Office" is hereby created
34 within the office of administration. The commissioner of
35 administration shall serve as the director of the hope
36 Missouri office.
37 (2) The office shall:
38 (a) Establish goals and strategies to accomplish the
39 mission of hope Missouri;
40 (b) Facilitate coordination and collaboration among
41 participating state agencies to achieve these goals;
42 (c) Expand the hope Missouri partner network to meet
43 the needs of participants;
44 (d) Empower and assist participants in need to help
45 identify and achieve immediate, intermediate, and long-term
46 goals and remove barriers to personal goal achievement
47 through the development and implementation of care plans in
48 partnership with hope navigators;
SB 1062 3
49 (e) Use hope navigators to assist participants in
50 identifying personal goals, developing individualized plans
51 for achieving immediate, intermediate, and long-term goals,
52 identifying and addressing barriers, and actively connecting
53 participants to community resources and opportunities to
54 achieve their goals;
55 (f) Use participating state agencies to serve families
56 holistically in achieving self-sufficiency, maximizing
57 community integration, and building a prosperous future.
58 Barriers to self-sufficiency may include the inability to:
59 a. Obtain stable employment;
60 b. Increase wages;
61 c. Obtain the necessary skills for greater
62 independence, education, or training;
63 d. Meet basic needs; and
64 e. Find stable housing;
65 (g) Refer participants requiring assistance with
66 employment or vocational training to appropriate entities to
67 prepare, train, and place the participants in meaningful
68 employment.
69 (3) (a) To participate in hope Missouri, an
70 individual shall be:
71 a. A legal resident of this state;
72 b. A citizen of the United States or a permanent
73 resident alien of the United States, as determined by the
74 United States Bureau of Citizenship and Immigration Services;
75 c. (i) At least eighteen years of age;
76 (ii) If emancipated, at least sixteen years of age; or
77 (iii) If under eighteen years of age and not
78 emancipated, authorized to participate by written consent of
79 his or her parent or guardian.
80 (b) Participation in hope Missouri is voluntary.
SB 1062 4
81 (c) Individuals may receive assistance by calling the
82 hope line.
83 (4) The office shall designate a state agency to, at a
84 minimum:
85 (a) Operate the hope line, which shall be available,
86 at a minimum, during business hours Monday through Friday;
87 (b) Develop and maintain a website for individuals to
88 connect with hope Missouri;
89 (c) Develop and maintain a hope Missouri case
90 management system that, at a minimum, conducts intake for
91 hope Missouri inquiries and makes referrals for individuals
92 and families in need of services.
93 4. (1) (a) Hope navigators shall serve as resources
94 who shall be embedded within communities and serve as
95 catalysts and a single point of contact for participants.
96 Hope navigators shall use participant information to jointly
97 develop and implement care plans and navigate and actively
98 leverage community-based supports and opportunities to
99 create a sustainable network of supports to help
100 participants achieve their goals, overcome barriers, and
101 realize their full potential.
102 (b) A care plan shall, at a minimum, include
103 actionable steps and corresponding timelines to address
104 immediate, intermediate, and long-term goals and barriers to
105 achieving such goals to help participants achieve their
106 personal goals. Personal goals may include goals related to
107 barriers to self-sufficiency.
108 (2) The hope Missouri partner network shall work to
109 build a network of partnerships to sustain the effectiveness
110 of hope Missouri.
SB 1062 5
111 (3) (a) State agencies shall participate in hope
112 Missouri at the direction of the commissioner of
113 administration.
114 (b) In order to use government resources more
115 effectively and efficiently, participating state agencies
116 shall use existing resources and personnel, to the extent
117 possible, to operate hope Missouri.
