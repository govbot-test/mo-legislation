Section 1:
SECOND REGULAR SESSION
103RD GENERA L ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY SENATOR BURGER.
5446S.01I KRISTINA MARTIN, Secretary
AN ACT
To repeal section 307.179, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof one new section relating to child
passenger restraint systems, with penalty provisions.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Section 307.179, RSMo, is repealed and one new
2 section enacted in lieu thereof, to be known as section 307.179,
3 to read as follows:
1 307.179. 1. As used in this section, the following
2 terms shall mean:
3 (1) "Child booster seat", a seating system which meets
4 the Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards set forth in 49
5 C.F.R. 571.213, as amended, that is designed to elevate a
6 child to properly sit in a federally approved safety belt
7 system;
8 (2) "Child passenger restraint system", a seating
9 system which meets the Federal Motor Vehicle Safety
10 Standards set forth in 49 C.F.R. 571.213, as amended, and
11 which is either permanently affixed to a motor vehicle or is
12 affixed to such vehicle by a safety belt or a universal
13 attachment system;
14 (3) "Driver", a person who is in actual physical
15 control of a motor vehicle.
16 2. Every driver transporting a child under the age of
17 sixteen years shall be responsible, when transporting such
EXPLANATION-Matter enclosed in bold-faced brackets [thus] in this bill is not enacted
and is intended to be omitted in the law.

18 child in a motor vehicle operated by that driver on the
19 streets or highways of this state, for providing for the
20 protection of such child as follows:
21 (1) Children shall be secured in a rear-facing child
22 passenger restraint system until the child reaches two years
23 of age;
24 (2) Children less than four years of age, regardless
25 of weight, shall be secured in a child passenger restraint
26 system appropriate for that child;
27 [(2)] (3) Children weighing less than forty pounds,
28 regardless of age, shall be secured in a child passenger
29 restraint system appropriate for that child;
30 [(3)] (4) Children at least four years of age but less
31 than eight years of age, who also weigh at least forty
32 pounds but less than eighty pounds, and who are also less
33 than four feet, nine inches tall, shall be secured in a
34 child passenger restraint system or booster seat appropriate
35 for that child;
36 [(4)] (5) Children at least eighty pounds or children

39 that child;
40 [(5)] (6) A child who otherwise would be required to
41 be secured in a booster seat may be transported in the back
42 seat of a motor vehicle while wearing only a lap belt if the
43 back seat of the motor vehicle is not equipped with a
44 combination lap and shoulder belt for booster seat
45 installation;
46 [(6)] (7) When transporting children in the immediate

50 restraint device appropriate for the child shall sit in the
51 area behind the front seat of the motor vehicle unless the
52 motor vehicle is designed only for a front seat area. The
53 driver transporting children referred to in this subsection
54 is not in violation of this section.
55 This subsection shall only apply to the use of a child
56 passenger restraint system or vehicle safety belt for
57 children less than sixteen years of age being transported in
58 a motor vehicle.
59 3. Any driver who violates subdivision (1), (2), [or]
60 (3), or (4) of subsection 2 of this section is guilty of an
61 infraction and, upon conviction, may be punished by a fine
62 of not more than fifty dollars and court costs. Any driver
63 who violates subdivision [(4)] (5) of subsection 2 of this
64 section shall be subject to the penalty in subsection 6 of
65 section 307.178. If a driver receives a citation for
66 violating subdivision (1), (2), [or] (3), or (4) of
67 subsection 2 of this section, the charges shall be dismissed
68 or withdrawn if the driver prior to or at his or her hearing
69 provides evidence of acquisition of a child passenger
70 restraint system or child booster seat which is satisfactory
71 to the court or the party responsible for prosecuting the
72 driver's citation.
73 4. The provisions of this section shall not apply to
74 any public carrier for hire. The provisions of this section
75 shall not apply to students four years of age or older who
76 are passengers on a school bus designed for carrying eleven
77 passengers or more and which is manufactured or equipped
78 pursuant to Missouri Minimum Standards for School Buses as
79 school buses are defined in section 301.010.
SB 1007 4
80 5. The highways and transportation commission shall
81 initiate and develop a program of public information to
82 develop understanding of, and ensure compliance with, the
83 provisions of this section.
