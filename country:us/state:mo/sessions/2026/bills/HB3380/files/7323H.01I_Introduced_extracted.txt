HOUSE BILL NO. 3380
103RD GENERAL ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY REPRESENTATIVE LUCAS.
7323H.01I JOSEPH ENGLER, Chief Clerk
AN ACT
To amend chapter 221, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to visitors to jails.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Chapter 221, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto one new section, to be
2 known as section 221.356, to read as follows:
221.356. 1. The provisions of this section shall be known and may be referred to
2 as the "No More Jail Deaths Act".
3 2. Except as provided in subsections 3, 4, and 5 of this section, no person shall be
4 permitted to enter a jail or detention facility of a city not within a county except by
5 special permission of the chief administrative officer of the jail or detention facility
6 under rules or regulations as such officer shall prescribe.
7 3. The following persons shall be authorized to visit the jails or detention
8 facilities of a city not within a county at any time:
9 (1) Authorized employees of the city not within a county;
10 (2) The chief executive officer of the city not within a county;
11 (3) The governor;
12 (4) The lieutenant governor;
13 (5) The attorney general;
14 (6) The state auditor;
15 (7) The state treasurer;
16 (8) The secretary of state;
17 (9) Members of the general assembly;
18 (10) The director of the department of public safety;

19 (11) The commissioners of the department of elementary and secondary
20 education;
21 (12) The commissioners of the department of higher education and workforce
22 development;
23 (13) The adjutant general of the state;
24 (14) Judges of the supreme court, courts of appeal, and circuit courts; and

26 4. The assistants of any person named in subsection 3 of this section shall be
27 authorized to visit a jail or detention facility of a city not within a county at any
28 reasonable time as specifically authorized by their superiors and in accordance with
29 rules and regulations established by the chief administrative officer of the jail or
30 detention facility.
31 5. All clergy of every recognized denomination shall be authorized to visit a jail
32 or detention facility of a city not within a county and may visit any offender confined in
33 any such jail or detention facility, subject to such rules or regulations as may be deemed
34 necessary for maintaining security and safety in the jail or detention facility. Such
35 clergy may administer the rites and ceremonies of the church to which they belong, if
36 such offender desires it and it is in compliance with rules or regulations of the jail or
37 detention facility.
38 6. The governing authority of a city not within a county that operates a jail or
39 detention facility shall adopt or update rules or regulations consistent with this section
40 no later than March 1, 2027, and submit a copy thereof to the department of public
41 safety for archival purposes. The department of public safety shall have authority to
42 provide guidance and may review the rules or regulations for consistency with state
43 standards.
