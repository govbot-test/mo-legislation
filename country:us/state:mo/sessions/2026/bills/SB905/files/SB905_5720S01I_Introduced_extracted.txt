Section 1:
SECOND REGULAR SESSION
103RD GENERA L ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY SENATOR GREGORY (15).
5720S.01I KRISTINA MARTIN, Secretary
AN ACT
To amend chapter 590, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to a training program
for Missouri rangers.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Chapter 590, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto
2 one new section, to be known as section 590.1300, to read as
3 follows:
1 590.1300. 1. The POST commission shall establish a
2 training program to be known as the "Missouri Rangers", and
3 shall establish minimum standards for training instructors,
4 training centers, and training programs that focus on
5 preventing and responding to emergency or violent crisis
6 situations in school settings.
7 2. (1) Each person seeking entrance into the Missouri
8 rangers training program shall submit a fingerprint card and
9 authorization for a criminal history background check to
10 include the records of the Federal Bureau of Investigation
11 to the training center or training program where such person
12 is seeking entrance. The training center or training
13 program shall cause a criminal history background check to
14 be made and shall cause the resulting report to be forwarded
15 to the agency where the applicant is seeking to be
16 designated as a Missouri ranger.
SB 905 2
17 (2) No person shall be admitted to a Missouri ranger
18 training center or training program unless such person
19 submits proof to the training center or training program
20 that he or she has a valid concealed carry endorsement or
21 permit.
22 3. The arrest powers granted to any person who
23 successfully completes the Missouri rangers training program
24 shall be limited to offenses established under chapter 571
25 and any offense under chapter 569.140 or 569.155 involving
26 school property. The provisions of this subsection shall
27 not apply to any person who is an active law enforcement
28 officer.
29 4. The POST commission shall develop a training
30 program that shall include:
31 (1) For applicants who are not active or retired law
32 enforcement or military personnel, a program that is not
33 longer than three months, and that shall consist of:
34 (a) State and federal constitutional and statutory law
35 relating to search and seizure;
36 (b) Firearms training;
37 (c) Defensive tactics; and
38 (d) Physical training, provided that no person shall
39 receive a credential under this subdivision unless such
40 person is able to:
41 a. Run one and one-half miles in less than twelve
42 minutes and thirty seconds;
43 b. Complete a minimum of four pull-ups;
44 c. Complete a minimum of forty pushups in less than
45 one minute; and
46 d. Complete fifty crunches in less than one minute;
47 (2) For applicants who are active or retired military
48 personnel, including active or retired members of the
SB 905 3
49 National Guard, a program that is not longer than two
50 months, and that shall consist of:
51 (a) State and federal constitutional and statutory law
52 relating to search and seizure;
53 (b) Abbreviated firearms training;
54 (c) Abbreviated defensive tactics; and
55 (d) Abbreviated physical training, provided that no
56 person shall receive a credential under this subdivision
57 unless such person is able to:
58 a. Run one and one-half miles in less than fourteen
59 minutes;
60 b. Complete a minimum of three pull-ups;
61 c. Complete a minimum of thirty pushups in less than
62 one minute; and
63 d. Complete forty crunches in less than one minute;
64 (3) For applicants who are active or retired law
65 enforcement personnel, a program that is not longer than six
66 weeks, and that shall consist of:
67 (a) Abbreviated state and federal constitutional and
68 statutory law relating to search and seizure;
69 (b) Abbreviated firearms training;
70 (c) Abbreviated defensive tactics; and
71 (d) Abbreviated physical training.
72 5. A certificate of Missouri ranger training program
73 completion may be issued to any applicant by any approved
74 Missouri ranger training instructor. On the certificate of
75 program completion, the approved Missouri ranger training
76 instructor shall affirm that the individual receiving
77 instruction has taken and passed a Missouri ranger training
78 program that meets the requirements of this section and
79 shall indicate whether the individual has a valid concealed
80 carry endorsement or permit. The instructor shall also
SB 905 4
81 provide a copy of such certificate to the director of the
82 department of public safety.
