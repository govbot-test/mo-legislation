Section 1:
SECOND REGULAR SESSION
103RD GENERAL ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY REPRESENTATIVE BURTON.
4658H.01I JOSEPH ENGLER, Chief Clerk
AN ACT
To repeal section 452.300, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof one new section relating to
petitions for dissolution of marriage, legal separation, or declaration of invalidity of
marriage.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:

Section 2:
Section A. Section 452.300, RSMo, is repealed and one new section enacted in lieu
2 thereof, to be known as section 452.300, to read as follows:

Section 3:
452.300. 1. The rules of the supreme court and other applicable court rules shall
2 govern all proceedings pursuant to sections 452.300 to 452.415.
3 2. A proceeding for dissolution of marriage, legal separation, or declaration of
4 invalidity of marriage shall be entitled: "In re the Marriage of ______ and ______".
5 3. The initial pleading in an original proceeding pursuant to sections 452.300 to
6 452.415 shall be denominated a "petition" and the responsive pleading in an original
7 proceeding shall be denominated an "answer". Other pleadings in an original proceeding and
8 all pleadings in other proceedings pursuant to sections 452.300 to 452.415 shall be
9 denominated as provided in the rules of the supreme court and other applicable court rules.
10 4. Any party who files the initial pleading in an original proceeding pursuant to
11 sections 452.300 to 452.415 shall be denominated the "petitioner" and any party who is
12 required to file or who files a responsive pleading in an original proceeding shall be
13 denominated the "respondent". Each party shall retain such denomination from the original
14 proceeding in any other proceedings pursuant to sections 452.300 to 452.415.
15 5. An original proceeding pursuant to sections 452.300 to 452.415 shall be
16 commenced in the county in which the petitioner resides or in the county in which the

17 respondent resides. If an original proceeding is commenced in the county in which the
18 petitioner resides, upon motion by the respondent filed prior to the filing of a responsive
19 pleading, the court in which the proceeding is commenced may transfer the proceeding to the
20 county in which the respondent resides if:
21 (1) The county in which the respondent resides had been the county in which the
22 children resided during the ninety days immediately preceding the commencement of the

24 (2) The best interest of the children will be served if the proceeding is transferred to

32 7. Any minor who was legally married prior to August 28, 2025, may file a

35 parent or guardian.
