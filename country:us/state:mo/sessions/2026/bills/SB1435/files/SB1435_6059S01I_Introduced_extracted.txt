Section 1:
SECOND REGULAR SESSION
103RD GENERA L ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY SENATOR WASHINGTON.
6059S.01I KRISTINA MARTIN, Secretary
AN ACT
To repeal section 286.010, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof one new section relating to the labor
and industrial relations commission.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Section 286.010, RSMo, is repealed and one new
2 section enacted in lieu thereof, to be known as section 286.010,
3 to read as follows:
1 286.010. There is hereby created and established a
2 separate department of the state government to be known as
3 "The Department of Labor and Industrial Relations". Such
4 department shall be under the control, management and
5 supervision of a commission to be known and designated as
6 "The Labor and Industrial Relations Commission of
7 Missouri". Such commission shall consist of [three] five
8 members, each of whom shall have resided in this state for
9 at least five years immediately prior to the person's
10 appointment, to be appointed by the governor, by and with
11 the advice and consent of the senate. One member of the
12 commission shall be a person who, on account of the person's
13 previous vocation, employment, affiliation or interests
14 shall be classified as a representative of employers, and
15 one member who, on account of the person's previous
16 vocation, employment, affiliation or interests shall be
17 classified as a representative of employees, and one member,
EXPLANATION-Matter enclosed in bold-faced brackets [thus] in this bill is not enacted
and is intended to be omitted in the law.
SB 1435 2
18 who, by reason of the person's previous activities and
19 interests shall be classified as a representative of the
20 public and who is licensed to practice law in the state of
21 Missouri; provided, however, that not more than [two] three
22 members of the commission shall be of the same political
23 party. A member of the commission shall be designated by
24 the governor as the chairman of the commission. During the
25 member's term of membership on the commission, no member
26 shall engage in any other business, vocation or employment,
27 or serve as an officer or committee member of any political
28 party or organization. Any [two] three commissioners shall
29 constitute a quorum. The governor shall fill any vacancy
30 within sixty days but no vacancy shall impair the right of
31 the remaining commissioners to exercise all powers of the
32 commission.
