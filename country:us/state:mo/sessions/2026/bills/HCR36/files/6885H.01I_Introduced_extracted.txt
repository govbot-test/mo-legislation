SECOND REGULAR SESSION
HOUSE CONCURRENT
RESOLUTION NO. 36
103RD GENERAL ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY REPRESENTATIVE SMITH (68).
6885H.01I JOSEPH ENGLER, Chief Clerk
AN ACT
Relating to Josephine Baker Day.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
WHEREAS, Josephine Baker was born in St. Louis, Missouri, on June 3, 1906; and
2
3 WHEREAS, Baker was a celebrated performer, headlining revues in Paris after
4 getting her start in St. Louis vaudeville acts; and
5
6 WHEREAS, Baker was the first black woman to star in a major motion picture, in
7 1927; and
8
9 WHEREAS, Baker participated extensively in the French Resistance during World
10 War II, and also aided the British Secret Intelligence Service and the United States Office of
11 Strategic Services:
12
13 NOW THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED that the members of the House of
14 Representatives of the One Hundred Third General Assembly, Second Regular Session, the
15 Senate concurring therein, hereby designate June 3rd of each year as "Josephine Baker Day"
16 in Missouri, in celebration of the legacy of Josephine Baker, a global icon in the arts, human
17 rights activism, and democracy; and
18
19 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that this resolution be sent to the Governor for his
20 approval or rejection pursuant to the Missouri Constitution.
