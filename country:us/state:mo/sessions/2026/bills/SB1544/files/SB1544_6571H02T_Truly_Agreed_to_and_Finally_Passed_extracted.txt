6571H.02T
AN ACT
To amend chapters 9, 10, 227, and 620, RSMo, by adding thereto thirty-eight new sections relating
to state designations.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Chapters 9, 10, 227, and 620, RSMo, are amended
2 by adding thereto thirty-eight new sections, to be known as
3 sections 9.025, 9.358, 9.406, 9.413, 9.418, 9.501, 9.502,
4 9.504, 9.509, 9.510, 9.516, 10.281, 10.282, 10.283, 10.284,
5 10.287, 10.288, 10.289, 10.292, 227.868, 227.895, 227.898,
6 227.900, 227.901, 227.902, 227.903, 227.904, 227.905, 227.906,
7 227.907, 227.908, 227.909, 227.910, 227.911, 227.912, 227.913,
8 227.914, and 620.430, to read as follows:
1 9.025. The month of January is hereby designated as
2 "Blood Donor Awareness Month". It is recommended to the
3 people of this state that the month be appropriately
4 observed through activities that increase awareness of the
5 importance of blood donation. The state deems blood and
6 blood products to be a critical resource and a vital public
7 health asset that must be readily available at all times.
1 9.358. April twenty-second each year is hereby
2 designated as "Missouri Black Bear Awareness Day". Citizens
3 of this state are encouraged to participate in appropriate
HCS SB 1544 2
4 events and activities to provide education about efforts to
5 conserve Missouri's black bear population.
1 9.406. April twenty-seventh each year is hereby
2 designated as "Ulysses S. Grant Day" in Missouri. The
3 citizens of this state are encouraged to participate in
4 appropriate events and activities to celebrate the life of
5 the Civil War general and eighteenth president of the United
6 States, who made his home in St. Louis, Missouri, in the
7 years following his graduation from West Point and before
8 the Civil War.
1 9.413. November twenty-fourth each year is hereby
2 designated as "Frankie Muse Freeman Day" in Missouri. The
3 citizens of this state are encouraged to participate in
4 appropriate events and activities to celebrate Frankie Muse
5 Freeman, a St. Louis attorney who was a champion for civil
6 rights, won a landmark case that ended legal racial
7 discrimination in St. Louis public housing, and was the
8 first black woman appointed to the United States Commission
9 on Civil Rights by president Lyndon B. Johnson.
1 9.418. The last full week of April each year shall be
2 known as "Infertility Awareness Week" in Missouri. It is
3 estimated that approximately one in eight couples in the
4 United States experience infertility, impacting people
5 across all racial, ethnic, socioeconomic, and cultural
6 backgrounds. The citizens of this state are encouraged to
7 participate in appropriate events and activities to raise
8 awareness about infertility.
1 9.501. The first full week in September each year is
2 hereby designated as "June's Week" and "Rare Pediatric
3 Disease Week" in Missouri in honor of June, a young
4 Missourian undergoing treatment for an atypical teratoid
5 rhabdoid tumor, subtype B (ATRT-B). The citizens of this
HCS SB 1544 3
6 state are encouraged to participate in appropriate events
7 and activities to raise public awareness of rare pediatric
8 diseases, encourage early recognition of symptoms, and honor
9 Missouri children and families affected by rare pediatric
10 diseases.
1 9.502. March twenty-sixth of each year is hereby
2 designated as "Pediatric Acute-Onset Neuropsychiatric
3 Syndrome (PANS)/Pediatric Autoimmune Neuropsychiatric
4 Disorder Associated with Streptococcus (PANDAS) Awareness
5 Day". The citizens of this state are encouraged to
6 participate in appropriate events and activities to raise
7 PANS/PANDAS awareness.
1 9.504. April fifth each year is hereby designated as
2 "Racquetball Day" in Missouri, in honor of Kirkwood
3 Racquetball, USA Racquetball's five-time consecutive high
4 school national champions. The citizens of this state are
5 encouraged to participate in appropriate events and
6 activities to learn more about the sport of racquetball and
7 celebrate Missouri's racquetball accomplishments.
1 9.509. April thirtieth each year is hereby designated
2 as "William Lacy Clay Sr. Day" in Missouri. The citizens of
3 this state are encouraged to participate in appropriate
4 events and activities to celebrate the life and legacy of
5 William Lacy Clay Sr., who represented Missouri's first
6 district in Congress from 1969 to 2001 and was one of the
7 founding members of the Congressional Black Caucus.
1 9.510. May tenth each year is hereby designated as
2 "Missouri River Runner Day" in Missouri. The citizens of
3 this state are encouraged to participate in appropriate
4 events and activities to recognize the role of passenger
5 rail in connecting Missouri communities, supporting economic
HCS SB 1544 4
6 development, and promoting safe and reliable transportation
7 across the state.
1 9.516. June twenty-eighth each year is hereby
2 designated as "Eliot and Muriel Battle Day" in Missouri.
3 The citizens of this state are encouraged to participate in
4 appropriate events and activities to celebrate the lives of
5 Eliot Battle, the first Black faculty member at Hickman High
6 School in Columbia, and Muriel Battle, the first Black
7 principal in the Columbia Public School District. The
8 couple worked as a team to ease racial tensions during the
9 1960s and have been recognized with schools in Columbia
10 named in their honor: Eliot Battle Elementary School and
11 Muriel Williams Battle High School.
1 10.281. The smooth chanterelle, scientifically known
2 as Cantharellus lateritius, is selected for and shall be
3 known as the official mushroom for the state of Missouri.
1 10.282. The city of Dexter is selected for and shall
2 be known as the official "Rib City" for the state of
3 Missouri.
1 10.283. Missouri Military Academy in the city of
2 Mexico is selected for and shall be known as the official
3 military academy of the state of Missouri.
1 10.284. The Missouri Cherry Blossom Festival in the
2 city of Marshfield is selected for and shall be known as the
3 official state cherry blossom festival for the state of
4 Missouri.
1 10.287. The city of Kansas City is selected for and
2 shall be known as the official "Barbecue Capital" of the
3 state of Missouri.
1 10.288. The city of Belle is selected for and shall be
2 known as the official "Cowbell Capital" of the state of
3 Missouri.
HCS SB 1544 5
1 10.289. The city of Bland is selected for and shall be
2 officially known as "The Silver City of the Rock Island
3 Trail" for the state of Missouri.
1 10.292. If the Kansas City Chiefs relocate outside of
2 this state, the St. Louis Battlehawks shall be the official
3 professional football team of the state of Missouri.
1 227.868. The portion of State Highway E from State
2 Highway 79 continuing east to Riverview Drive in Ralls
3 County shall be designated the "James Thompson Highway".
4 The department of transportation shall erect and maintain
5 appropriate signs designating such highway, with the costs
6 to be paid by private donations.
1 227.895. The portion of State Highway D from State
2 Highway C continuing north to State Highway Y in St.
3 Francois County shall be designated as the "Carl G Koester
4 Memorial Highway". The department of transportation shall
5 erect and maintain signs designating such highway, with the
6 costs to be paid by private donations.
1 227.898. The portion of State Highway WW from State
2 Highway 13 continuing to State Highway H in Greene County
3 shall be designated the "Gary D McCormack Nemo Macto Unus
4 Memorial Highway". The department of transportation shall
5 erect and maintain appropriate signs designating such
6 highway, with the costs to be paid by the department of
7 transportation.
1 227.900. The bridge on CST Dougherty Ferry Road
2 crossing over Interstate 270 in St. Louis County shall be
3 designated the "Army SSG Salvatore Palazzolo Memorial
4 Bridge". The department of transportation shall erect and
5 maintain appropriate signs designating such bridge, with the
6 costs to be paid by the department of transportation.
HCS SB 1544 6
1 227.901. The portion of U.S. Highway 24 from Davis
2 Road continuing east to Ferguson Spring Road in Jackson
3 County shall be designated the "WWII Robert Earl Sauls

5 erect and maintain appropriate signs designating such
6 highway, with the costs to be paid by private donations.
