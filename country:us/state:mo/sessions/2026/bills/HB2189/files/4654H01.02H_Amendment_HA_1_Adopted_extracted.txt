4654H01.02H
HB 2189
Offered By
_____________________________________ ___________________________________
1 AMEND House Bill No. 2189, Page 1, Section A, Line 2, by inserting after all of said section
2 and line the following:
3
4 "136.055. 1. Any person who is selected or appointed by the state director of revenue as
5 provided in subsection 2 of this section to act as an agent of the department of revenue, whose
6 duties shall be the processing of motor vehicle title and registration transactions and the
7 collection of sales and use taxes when required under sections 144.070 and 144.440, and who
8 receives no salary from the department of revenue, shall be authorized to collect from the party
9 requiring such services additional fees as compensation in full and for all services rendered on
10 the following basis:
11 (1) For each motor vehicle registration issued, renewed, or transferred:
12 (a) Nine dollars for annual registration; [or]
13 (b) Eighteen dollars for biennial registration; or
14 (c) Forty-five dollars for five-year registration;
15 (2) For each trailer registration issued, renewed, or transferred:
16 (a) Nine dollars for annual registration;
17 (b) Twenty-seven dollars for three-year registration; or
18 (c) Forty-five dollars for permanent registration;
19 (3) For each application or transfer of title, nine dollars;
20 (4) For each instruction permit, nondriver license, chauffeur's, operator's or driver's
21 license issued for a period of three years or less, nine dollars and eighteen dollars for licenses or
22 instruction permits issued or renewed for a period exceeding three years;
23 (5) For each notice of lien processed, nine dollars;
24 (6) Notary fee or electronic transmission per processing, two dollars.
25 2. The director of revenue shall award fee office contracts under this section through a
26 competitive bidding process. The competitive bidding process shall give priority to
27 organizations and entities that are exempt from taxation under Section 501(c)(3), 501(c)(6), or
28 501(c)(4), except those civic organizations that would be considered action organizations under
29 26 C.F.R. Section 1.501 (c)(3)-1(c)(3), of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended, with
30 special consideration given to those organizations and entities that reinvest a minimum of
31 seventy-five percent of the net proceeds to charitable organizations in Missouri, and political
32 subdivisions, including but not limited to, municipalities, counties, and fire protection districts.
33 Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the director of revenue shall not award any
34 fee office contract under this section to any entity affiliated in any manner with a current
35 employee of the department of revenue or with a former employee of the department of revenue
36 for the one-year period following the former employee's termination of employment with the
Action Taken___________________________________________ Date __________________
Page 1 of 2
4654H01.02H
1 department. For purposes of this subsection, "affiliated in any manner" includes owning the
2 entity or serving as an officer or board member of such entity. Additionally, no person affiliated
3 in any manner with an entity awarded a fee office contract under this section shall be affiliated in
4 any manner with an entity acting as a motor vehicle title service agent as prescribed in sections
5 301.112 to 301.119. The director of the department of revenue may promulgate rules and
6 regulations necessary to carry out the provisions of this subsection. Any rule or portion of a rule,
7 as that term is defined in section 536.010, that is created under the authority delegated in this
8 subsection shall become effective only if it complies with and is subject to all of the provisions
9 of chapter 536 and, if applicable, section 536.028. This section and chapter 536 are
10 nonseverable and if any of the powers vested with the general assembly pursuant to chapter 536
11 to review, to delay the effective date, or to disapprove and annul a rule are subsequently held
12 unconstitutional, then the grant of rulemaking authority and any rule proposed or adopted after
13 August 28, 2009, shall be invalid and void.
14 3. Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, the director of revenue
15 shall have the authority to enter into a contract amendment or renewal, for any contract for a fee
16 office awarded through the competitive bidding process after September 1, 2009, to extend such
17 contract for up to a five-year period to begin after the expiration date of such contract. The
18 director of revenue shall evaluate performance under the contract when deciding whether to
19 enter into contract amendments or renewals authorized in this subsection. Nothing shall obligate
20 the director to offer such extension or renewal. A competitively awarded contract may only be
21 extended once pursuant to this subsection.
22 4. All fees authorized under this section collected by a contract fee office may be
23 retained and used by the entity operating the contract fee office, and all fees authorized under
24 this section collected by a fee office operated by the department of revenue shall be considered
25 state revenue.
26 5. All fees charged shall not exceed those in this section. The fees authorized under this
27 section shall be collected by all contract fee offices and shall be collected by all full-time or
28 temporary offices operated by the department of revenue.
29 6. Any person acting as agent of the department of revenue for the sale and issuance of
30 registrations, licenses, and other documents related to motor vehicles shall have an insurable
31 interest in all license plates, licenses, tabs, forms and other documents held on behalf of the
32 department.
33 7. The fees authorized by this section shall not be collected by motor vehicle dealers
34 acting as agents of the department of revenue under section 32.095 or those motor vehicle
35 dealers authorized to collect and remit sales tax under subsection 10 of section 144.070.
36 8. Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, the state auditor may audit
37 all records maintained and established by the fee office in the same manner as the auditor may
38 audit any agency of the state, and the department shall ensure that this audit requirement is a
39 necessary condition for the award of all fee office contracts. No confidential records shall be
40 divulged in such a way to reveal personally identifiable information."; and
41
42 Further amend said bill by amending the title, enacting clause, and intersectional references
43 accordingly.
