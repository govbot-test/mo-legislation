HOUSE BILL NO. 3373
103RD GENERAL ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY REPRESENTATIVE DOUGLAS.
7304H.01I JOSEPH ENGLER, Chief Clerk
AN ACT
To amend chapter 173, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to support for
homeless pupils through the department of higher education and workforce
development.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Chapter 173, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto one new section, to be
2 known as section 173.2541, to read as follows:
173.2541. 1. As used in this section, the following terms mean:
2 (1) "Homeless pupils", includes any person who satisfies the definition of
3 "homeless children and youths" established in 42 U.S.C. Section 11434a(2) and any
4 person who satisfies the definition of "unaccompanied youth" established in 42 U.S.C.
5 Section 11434a(6);
6 (2) "Liaison", the liaison for postsecondary education for homeless pupils
7 established in this section.
8 2. There is hereby established the liaison for postsecondary education for
9 homeless pupils within the department of higher education and workforce development.
10 3. There is hereby created in the state treasury the "Liaison for Postsecondary
11 Education for Homeless Pupils Fund", which shall consist of moneys appropriated by
12 the general assembly or any gifts, donations, or bequests for the purpose of
13 implementing the provisions of this section. The state treasurer shall be custodian of
14 the fund. In accordance with sections 30.170 and 30.180, the state treasurer may
15 approve disbursements. The fund shall be a dedicated fund, and moneys in the fund
16 shall be used solely by the department of higher education and workforce development

17 for the purposes of implementing the provisions of this section. Notwithstanding the
18 provisions of section 33.080 to the contrary, any moneys remaining in the fund at the
19 end of the biennium shall not revert to the credit of the general revenue fund. The state
20 treasurer shall invest moneys in the fund in the same manner as other funds are
21 invested. Any interest and moneys earned on such investments shall be credited to the
22 fund.
23 4. The department of higher education and workforce development shall employ
24 a liaison for the purpose of implementing this section. The individual appointed:
25 (1) Shall be knowledgeable in the various issues relating to homeless pupils
26 including, but not limited to, the McKinney-Vento Homeless Assistance Act of 1987, 42
27 U.S.C. Section 11431 et seq.; and
28 (2) Shall be independent of and have no pecuniary interest in any other
29 organization or entity that provides services to homeless pupils.
30 5. Subject to appropriations, the liaison shall:
31 (1) Conduct an annual analysis of homeless pupils in this state to assess the needs
32 of such pupils;
33 (2) Develop a database to track, monitor, and analyze trends in the rates of
34 graduation and retention of homeless pupils;
35 (3) Develop a model for college and career readiness for homeless pupils;
36 (4) Collaborate with high school staff members responsible for assisting
37 homeless and unaccompanied pupils;
38 (5) Collaborate with high schools to identify homeless pupils to refer such pupils
39 to appropriate support services after the admission of such pupils to an institution of
40 higher education within this state;
41 (6) Establish connections between and collaborate with financial aid offices,
42 student support services, and campus housing services of institutions of higher
43 education within this state;
44 (7) Identify and refer homeless pupils to mentoring programs;
45 (8) Increase awareness among teachers, instructors, professors, and other staff
46 members of institutions of higher education in this state who work with pupils on issues
47 relating to homeless pupils including, without limitation, identifying such pupils and
48 referring such pupils to appropriate resources;
49 (9) Establish and maintain a database of food banks, clothing banks, and low-
50 cost health care providers, to be provided, without limitation, to financial aid offices and
51 student support services of institutions of higher education;
52 (10) Identify and coordinate with food banks and clothing banks within this state
53 to provide services to homeless pupils; and
