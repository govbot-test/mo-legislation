1 AMEND House Bill No. 1802, Page 3, Section 115.107, Line 32, by inserting after all of said
2 section and line the following:
3
4 "115.133. 1. Except as provided in subsection 2 of this section, any citizen of the United
5 States who is a resident of the state of Missouri and seventeen years and six months of age or
6 older shall be entitled to register and to vote in any election which is held on or after his or her
7 eighteenth birthday.
8 2. No person who is adjudged incapacitated shall be entitled to register or vote. No
9 person shall be entitled to vote:
10 (1) While confined under a sentence of imprisonment after conviction of a felony; or
11 (2) [While on probation or parole after conviction of a felony, until finally discharged
12 from such probation or parole; or
13 (3)] After conviction of a felony [or misdemeanor] connected with the right of suffrage.
14 3. Except as provided in federal law or federal elections and in section 115.277, no
15 person shall be entitled to vote if the person has not registered to vote in the jurisdiction of his or
16 her residence prior to the deadline to register to vote.
17 4. Nothing in this section shall prohibit any defendant who is otherwise eligible to vote
18 and is confined in a jail from voting in an election if he or she has not yet been convicted of a
19 crime.
20 115.155. 1. The election authority shall provide for the registration of each voter. Voter
21 registration applications shall be made available in English, Spanish, and Braille. Each
22 application shall be in substantially the following form:

1 2. The options for political party affiliation required by the application described in
2 subsection 1 of this section shall include all established political parties and an option to be
3 unaffiliated. If an applicant does not designate an affiliation, the election authority shall mark
4 the applicant's form as unaffiliated.
5 3. After supplying all information necessary for the registration records, each applicant
6 who appears in person before the election authority shall swear or affirm the statements on the
7 registration application by signing his or her full name, witnessed by the signature of the election
8 authority or such authority's deputy registration official. Each applicant who applies to register
9 by mail pursuant to section 115.159, or pursuant to section 115.160 or 115.162, shall attest to the
10 statements on the application by his or her signature.
11 4. Upon receipt by mail of a completed and signed voter registration application, a voter
12 registration application forwarded by the division of motor vehicle and drivers licensing of the
13 department of revenue pursuant to section 115.160, or a voter registration agency pursuant to
14 section 115.162, the election authority shall, if satisfied that the applicant is entitled to register,
15 transfer all data necessary for the registration records from the application to its registration
16 system. Within seven business days after receiving the application, the election authority shall
17 send the applicant a verification notice. If such notice is returned as undeliverable by the postal
18 service within the time established by the election authority, the election authority shall not place
19 the applicant's name on the voter registration file.
20 5. If, upon receipt by mail of a voter registration application or a voter registration
21 application forwarded pursuant to section 115.160 or 115.162, the election authority determines
22 that the applicant is not entitled to register, such authority shall, within seven business days after
23 receiving the application, so notify the applicant by mail and state the reason such authority has
24 determined the applicant is not qualified. The applicant may file a complaint with the elections
25 division of the secretary of state's office under and pursuant to section 115.219. If an applicant
26 for voter registration fails to answer the question on the application concerning United States
27 citizenship, the election authority shall notify the applicant of the failure and provide the
28 applicant with an opportunity to complete the form in a timely manner to allow for the
29 completion of the registration form before the next election.
30 6. The secretary of state shall prescribe specifications for voter registration documents so
31 that they are uniform throughout the state of Missouri and comply with the National Voter

1 Registration Act of 1993, including the reporting requirements, and so that registrations, name
2 changes and transfers of registrations within the state may take place as allowed by law.
3 7. All voter registration applications shall be preserved in the office of the election
4 authority.
5 115.247. 1. Each election authority shall provide all ballots for every election within its
6 jurisdiction. Ballots other than those printed by the election authority in accordance with the
7 provisions of this chapter shall not be cast or counted at any election.
8 2. Whenever it appears that an error has occurred in any publication required by the
9 provisions of this chapter, or in the printing of any ballot, any circuit court may, upon the
10 application of any voter, order the appropriate election authorities to correct the error or to show
11 cause why the error should not be corrected.
12 3. For each election held in a county with a charter form of government and with more
13 than two hundred fifty thousand but fewer than three hundred fifty thousand inhabitants, the
14 election authority may provide for each polling place in its jurisdiction fifty-five ballots for each
15 fifty and fraction of fifty voters registered in the voting district at the time of the election. For
16 each election, except a general election, held in any county other than a county with a charter
17 form of government and with more than two hundred fifty thousand but fewer than three
18 hundred fifty thousand inhabitants, the election authority shall provide for each polling place in
19 its jurisdiction a number of ballots equal to at least one and one-third times the number of ballots
20 cast in the voting district served by such polling place at the election held two years before at
21 that polling place or at the polling place that served the voting district in the previous election.
22 For each general election held in any county other than a county with a charter form of
23 government and with more than two hundred fifty thousand but fewer than three hundred fifty
24 thousand inhabitants, the election authority shall provide for each polling place in its jurisdiction
25 a number of ballots equal to one and one-third times the number of ballots cast in the voting
26 district served by such polling place or at the polling place that served the voting district in the
27 general election held four years prior. When determining the number of ballots to provide for
28 each polling place, the election authority shall consider any factors that would affect the turnout
29 at such polling place. The election authority shall keep a record of the exact number of ballots
30 delivered to each polling place. For purposes of this subsection, the election authority shall not
31 be required to count registered voters designated as inactive pursuant to section 115.193.
32 4. After the polls have closed on every election day, the election judges shall return all
33 unused ballots to the election authority with the other election supplies.
34 5. All ballots cast in public elections shall be printed and distributed at public expense,
35 payable as provided in sections 115.063 to 115.077.
36 6. The election authority shall emboss and make available upon request ballots in Braille
37 for use by voters who are visually impaired.

1 115.281. 1. Except as provided in section 115.914, not later than the sixth Tuesday prior
2 to each election, or within fourteen days after candidates' names or questions are certified
3 pursuant to section 115.125, the election authority shall cause to have printed and made available
4 a sufficient quantity of absentee ballots, ballot envelopes and mailing envelopes. As soon as
5 possible after the proper officer calls a special state or county election, the election authority
6 shall cause to have printed and made available a sufficient quantity of absentee ballots, ballot
7 envelopes and mailing envelopes.
8 2. All absentee ballots for an election shall be in the same form as the official ballots for
9 the election, including ballots in Braille as required in section 115.247.
10 115.287. 1. Upon receipt of a signed application for an absentee ballot and if satisfied
11 the applicant is entitled to vote by absentee ballot, the election authority shall, within three
12 working days after receiving the application, or if absentee ballots are not available at the time
13 the application is received, within five working days after they become available, deliver to the
14 voter an absentee ballot, ballot envelope and such instructions as are necessary for the applicant
15 to vote. Delivery shall be made to the voter personally in the office of the election authority or
16 by bipartisan teams appointed by the election authority, or by first class, registered, or certified
17 mail at the discretion of the election authority, or in the case of a covered voter as defined in
18 section 115.902, the method of transmission prescribed in section 115.914. Where the election
19 authority is a county clerk, the members of bipartisan teams representing the political party other
20 than that of county clerk shall be selected from a list of persons submitted to the county clerk by
21 the county chairman of that party. If no list is provided by the time that absentee ballots are to be
22 made available, the county clerk may select a person or persons from lists provided in
23 accordance with section 115.087. If the election authority is not satisfied that any applicant is
24 entitled to vote by absentee ballot, it shall not deliver an absentee ballot to the applicant. Within
25 three working days of receiving such an application, the election authority shall notify the
26 applicant and state the reason he or she is not entitled to vote by absentee ballot. The applicant
27 may file a complaint with the elections division of the secretary of state's office under and
28 pursuant to section 115.219.
29 2. If, after 5:00 p.m. on the second Wednesday before an election, any voter from the
30 jurisdiction is confined in a jail, has become hospitalized, becomes confined due to illness or
31 injury, or is confined in an intermediate care facility, residential care facility, or skilled nursing
32 facility on election day, as such terms are defined in section 198.006, in the county in which the
33 jurisdiction is located or in the jurisdiction of an adjacent election authority within the same
34 county, the election authority shall appoint a team to deliver, witness the signing of and return
35 the voter's application and deliver, witness the voting of and return the voter's absentee ballot. If
36 the election authority receives ten or more applications for absentee ballots from the same
37 address it shall appoint a team to deliver and witness the voting and return of absentee ballots by
38 voters residing at that address, except when such addresses are for an apartment building or other
Page 6 of 7
4063H01.02H
1 structure wherein individual living units are located, each of which has its own separate cooking
2 facilities. Each team appointed pursuant to this subsection shall consist of two registered voters,
3 one from each major political party. Both members of any team appointed pursuant to this
4 subsection shall be present during the delivery, signing or voting and return of any application or
5 absentee ballot signed or voted pursuant to this subsection.
6 3. On the mailing and ballot envelopes for each covered voter, the election authority
7 shall stamp prominently in black the words "FEDERAL BALLOT, STATE OF MISSOURI" and
8 "U.S. Postage Paid, 39 U.S.C. Section 3406".
9 4. No information which encourages a vote for or against a candidate or issue shall be
10 provided to any voter with an absentee ballot.
11 561.026. Notwithstanding any other provision of law except for section 610.140, a
12 person who is convicted:
13 (1) Of [any offense] a felony shall be disqualified from registering and voting in any
14 election under the laws of this state while confined under a sentence of imprisonment;
15 (2) Of a felony or misdemeanor connected with the exercise of the right of suffrage shall
16 be forever disqualified from registering and voting;
17 (3) Of any felony shall be forever disqualified from serving as a juror."; and

20 accordingly.
