Section 1:
SECOND REGULAR SESSION
103RD GENERA L ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY SENATOR BURGER.
6222S.01I KRISTINA MARTIN, Secretary
AN ACT
To repeal sections 643.263 and 643.265, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof one new section
relating to asbestos testing.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Sections 643.263 and 643.265, RSMo, are
2 repealed and one new section enacted in lieu thereof, to be
3 known as section 643.263, to read as follows:
1 643.263. 1. Each public school district and private
2 or parochial school in Missouri shall [report to the
3 department of health and senior services] maintain any
4 reports relating to the results of the examination of its
5 structures for friable asbestos as required by the
6 Environmental Protection Agency under the Toxic Substances
7 Control Act, PL 94-469. [All such reports shall be filed
8 with the department of health and senior services before
9 March 1, 1987.] Other political subdivisions shall [assess]
10 maintain assessments relating to asbestos in buildings
11 owned, leased or operated by the political subdivisions and
12 [report] reports relating to the results of the examination
13 of its structures for friable asbestos [to the department of
14 health and senior services by December 31, 1991]. The
15 results of the assessment shall be public information and
16 shall be readily available to the public and employees of
EXPLANATION-Matter enclosed in bold-faced brackets [thus] in this bill is not enacted
and is intended to be omitted in the law.

17 each political subdivision and each school district and

25 management of any friable asbestos. The report shall also

29 district and any private or parochial school which adopts
30 interim measures or which has not completed its management
31 plan shall reevaluate the material biennially and report its
32 findings [to the department of health and senior services
33 and make its findings available] to its employees and the
34 public. Any political subdivision, any school district and
35 any private or parochial school which identifies nonfriable
36 asbestos shall, no less often than every three years,
37 reevaluate the material and report its findings [to the
38 department of health and senior services and make its
39 findings available] to its employees and the public. [The
40 department of health and senior services shall provide
41 technical assistance including training, consultation and
42 inspection upon request to political subdivisions in
43 identifying buildings owned, leased or operated by political
44 subdivisions which have asbestos-containing materials.
45 3. The department of health and senior services shall
46 annually provide the department of elementary and secondary
47 education with a list of all public school districts whose
48 buildings contain friable asbestos.

1 [643.265. The department of health and
2 senior services and the department of natural
3 resources shall develop an interagency agreement
4 establishing agency responsibilities and
5 procedures concerning asbestos reporting,
6 removal and treatment. The department of health
7 and senior services is hereby designated the
8 state agency for the administration of loans and
9 grants under the Asbestos School Hazard
10 Abatement Act of 1984, Public Law 98-377, and
11 other federally funded loan or grant programs
12 for the abatement of asbestos.]
