SECOND REGULAR SESSION
SENATE BILL NO. 1776
103RD GENERA L ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY SENATOR COLEMAN.
6977S.01I KRISTINA MARTIN, Secretary
AN ACT
To repeal section 193.165, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof one new section relating to
spontaneous fetal death reports.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Section 193.165, RSMo, is repealed and one new
2 section enacted in lieu thereof, to be known as section 193.165,
3 to read as follows:
1 193.165. 1. Each spontaneous fetal death of twenty
2 completed weeks gestation or more, calculated from the date
3 the last normal menstrual period began to the date of
4 delivery, or a weight of three hundred fifty grams or more,
5 which occurs in this state shall be reported within seven
6 days after delivery to the local registrar or as otherwise
7 directed by the state registrar.
8 2. When a dead fetus is delivered in an institution,
9 the person in charge of the institution or his or her
10 designated representative shall prepare and file the report.
11 3. When a dead fetus is delivered outside an
12 institution, the physician in attendance at or immediately
13 after delivery shall prepare and file the report.
14 4. When a spontaneous fetal death required to be
15 reported by this section occurs without medical attendance
16 at or immediately after the delivery or when inquiry is
17 required by the medical examiner or coroner, the medical

18 examiner or coroner shall investigate the cause of
19 spontaneous fetal death and shall prepare and file the
20 report within seven days; provided, that when a licensed
21 treating physician has determined, in writing, the
22 nonviability of the pregnancy preceding the spontaneous
23 fetal death, such written determination shall serve as
24 sufficient evidence for purposes of this section and the
25 treating physician shall prepare and file the report within
26 seven days of receiving notice of the spontaneous fetal
27 death.
28 5. When a spontaneous fetal death occurs in a moving
29 conveyance and the fetus is first removed from the
30 conveyance in this state or when a dead fetus is found in
31 this state and the place of the spontaneous fetal death is
32 unknown, the spontaneous fetal death shall be reported in
33 this state. The place where the fetus was first removed
34 from the conveyance or the dead fetus was found shall be
35 considered the place of the spontaneous fetal death.
36 6. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the
37 contrary, individuals with direct and tangible interest, as
38 defined by the department of health and senior services, may
39 receive the spontaneous fetal death report.
40 7. In the event of a spontaneous fetal death,
41 regardless of whether such death occurs before or after
42 August 28, 2004, either parent, or if both parents are
43 deceased, a sibling of the stillborn child, shall have the
44 right to file an application with the state registrar and
45 other custodians of vital records requesting a certificate
46 of birth resulting in stillbirth. The certificate shall be
47 based upon the information available from the spontaneous
48 fetal death report filed pursuant to this section.
