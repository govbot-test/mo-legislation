Section 1:
SECOND REGULAR SESSION
103RD GENERAL ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY REPRESENTATIVE BYRNES.
7419H.01I JOSEPH ENGLER, Chief Clerk
AN ACT
To amend chapter 197, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to medically
complex pediatric patients.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:

Section 2:
Section A. Chapter 197, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto one new section, to be
2 known as section 197.181, to read as follows:

Section 3:
197.181. 1. This section shall be known and may be cited as "Maddie's Law: The
2 Rare Pediatric Disease Care and Protocol Protection Act".
3 2. As used in this section, the following terms mean:
4 (1) "Care plan", a patient-specific electronic document that may include
5 diagnoses, baseline clinical status, medications and allergies, medical devices, emergency
6 treatment recommendations, treating providers, and communication needs;
7 (2) "Department", the department of health and senior services;
8 (3) "Emergency department", an emergency department in a hospital licensed
9 under this chapter;
10 (4) "Medically complex pediatric patient", a person under eighteen years of age
11 who has one or more chronic medical conditions expected to last at least twelve months
12 and who requires specialty care, multiple medications, medical technology, or care
13 coordination across multiple providers.
14 3. (1) Each hospital that operates an emergency department in this state shall
15 implement, within its existing electronic health record system, a clearly visible electronic
16 alert or flag for a medically complex pediatric patient.
17 (2) The alert shall:
EXPLANATION — Matter enclosed in bold-faced brackets [thus] in the above bill is not enacted and is
intended to be omitted from the law. Matter in bold-face type in the above bill is proposed language.
HB 3457 2
18 (a) Appear in a manner that is immediately identifiable to emergency
19 department personnel on the patient tracking screen or within the patient's electronic
20 chart;
21 (b) Provide rapid, one-click access to the patient's care plan, if available; and
22 (c) Function in a manner that does not delay triage or treatment.
23 4. A hospital shall be considered in compliance with this section if the hospital
24 utilizes existing alert, flagging, or patient notification functionality within its current
25 electronic health record system.
26 5. Nothing in this section shall be construed to require a hospital to:
27 (1) Purchase a new electronic health record system;
28 (2) Develop a separate technology platform; or
29 (3) Create a new full-time position.
30 6. A parent or legal guardian of a medically complex pediatric patient may
31 request inclusion of the patient in the alert system and may submit a care plan for the
32 patient for inclusion in the hospital's electronic health record.
33 7. Any care plan for a medically complex pediatric patient shall be entered into
34 the system in coordination with a licensed health care provider.
35 8. Participation in the alert system shall be voluntary and shall require the
36 consent of the parent or legal guardian of the medically complex pediatric patient.
37 9. All information maintained under this section shall comply with applicable
38 state and federal privacy laws, including the federal Health Insurance Portability and
39 Accountability Act (HIPAA).
40 10. Any hospital or health care provider acting in good-faith reliance on a care
41 plan accessed in accordance with this section shall not be subject to additional civil
42 liability solely for such reliance.
43 11. The department shall administer the provisions of this section within existing
44 appropriations.
45 12. The department may issue model guidelines to assist hospitals in
46 implementation of the requirements of this section. Such guidelines shall be advisory
47 and shall not require formal rulemaking unless necessary.
48 13. Nothing in this section shall be construed to:
49 (1) Establish a state registry;
50 (2) Require the creation of a state database; or
51 (3) Require the department to collect or store patient-specific medical
52 information.
