AN ACT
To amend chapters 9 and 210, RSMo, by adding thereto two new sections relating to
fatherhood engagement.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Chapters 9 and 210, RSMo, are amended by adding thereto two new
2 sections, to be known as sections 9.411 and 210.1150, to read as follows:
9.411. The month of June is hereby designated as "Fathership Month" in the
2 state of Missouri. The citizens of this state are encouraged to engage in appropriate
3 events and activities to raise awareness about the importance of fathers being part of
4 their children's lives.
210.1150. 1. There is hereby established within the department of social services
2 the "Missouri Fathership Project". The Missouri fathership project shall be
3 administered by the department of social services, subject to federal approval and
4 state appropriations. The Missouri fathership project shall:
5 (1) Promote fathership by helping fathers achieve greater self-esteem and better
6 awareness of their responsibilities to their families and community; and
7 (2) Empower fathers to become successful in fathership by building life skills
8 and through family reunification, education and career training, employment support,
9 and child-support compliance.
10 2. (1) There is hereby established within the department of social services the
11 "Missouri Fathership Project Grant Program" to provide grants to agencies, programs,
12 or nonprofit organizations that support and facilitate involvement of fathers in the
13 community, subject to federal approval and state appropriations. The department shall

29 challenges that have caused a breakdown of their parenting relationships and help
30 fathers navigate the system to reunify with their children.
31 (c) Fathership project specialists shall:
32 a. Serve as a liaison between fathers and any relevant state agencies or
33 organizations, including the family court system and the Missouri family support
34 division;
35 b. Assist fathers in developing and implementing personalized plans to
36 reestablish and maintain a relationship with their children; and
37 c. Provide guidance, support, and resources to fathers to help fathers overcome
38 compliance barriers with court-ordered case plans and other obstacles preventing
39 reunification with their children.
40 (d) If a father ceases to engage with or comply with a court-ordered visitation
41 and custody order, the court shall issue a referral to a partnering agency, program, or
42 nonprofit organization utilizing services from a fathership project specialist. The
43 referring entity shall inform the agency, program, or nonprofit organization that the
44 father is no longer working toward the goal of reunification, and the agency, program,
45 or nonprofit organization shall dispatch a fathership project specialist to engage with
46 the father. If a father requests assistance through the Missouri family support division
47 or directly to a partnering agency, program, or nonprofit organization, the father shall
48 also be eligible for assistance from a fathership project specialist.
