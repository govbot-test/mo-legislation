AN ACT
To amend chapter 160, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to accountability
measures for elementary and secondary schools.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Chapter 160, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto one new section, to be
2 known as section 160.524, to read as follows:
160.524. 1. (1) The state board of education shall develop an annual school
2 accountability report card for each public school district, public school, and public
3 charter school in the state for the purpose of providing information about school
4 performance in accordance with the provisions of this section. In addition to providing
5 information about student performance, the report card shall be designed to satisfy
6 federal reporting requirements for the disclosure of information about students, staff,
7 finances, and other data.
8 (2) Each school accountability report card shall be designed in a standardized,
9 clear, and easily accessible form so that the accountability report card can be easily
10 understood by parents, taxpayers, school personnel, legislators, and the media and shall
11 include a summary intended for parents and guardians that:
12 (a) Explains the school's performance level and underlying indicators in
13 nontechnical terms;
14 (b) Identifies areas of strength and areas in need of improvement;
15 (c) Highlights year-to-year progress, including student academic growth; and
16 (d) Provides information on how families may engage with the school to support
17 student learning.

24 3. (1) The state board of education shall provide an embargoed version of the

29 (3) Before October thirty-first of each year, each public school district, public
30 school, and public charter school shall publish in a clear and easily accessible location

32 school year.
33 4. The state board of education shall assign each public school district, public
34 school, and public charter school one of the following ratings, which shall be based on a
35 zero-to-one-hundred scale:
36 (1) "A" for those districts and schools producing excellent student outcomes;
37 (2) "B" for those districts and schools producing more-than-satisfactory student
38 outcomes;
39 (3) "C" for those districts and schools producing satisfactory student outcomes;
40 (4) "D" for those districts and schools producing less-than-satisfactory student
41 outcomes; and
42 (5) "F" for those districts and schools failing to produce adequate student
43 outcomes.
44 5. (1) The state board of education shall assign each public school district,
45 public school, and public charter school an overall performance rating of "A", "B",
46 "C", "D", or "F", derived from multiple measures of school performance as provided
47 in this section.
48 (2) The benchmark for excellent student outcomes or an "A" rating shall be the
49 top decile of the composite score of the measures outlined in subsection 8 of this section.
50 (3) A public school district, public school, or public charter school that does not
51 test at least ninety-five percent of its students shall have assessment participation rates
52 reported separately and clearly identified on the school accountability report card.
53 Assessment participation rates shall not result in an automatic reduction of the district's
54 or school's performance level but shall be included to provide transparency and context
55 for parents and the public.

56 6. (1) There is hereby created the "Show Me Success Program" to provide
57 additional funding, subject to appropriation, to public schools and public charter
58 schools that demonstrate strong student academic growth, sustained improvement over
59 time, or success in closing achievement gaps, as determined by the department of
60 elementary and secondary education.
61 (2) Subject to appropriations, the department may award performance-based
62 funding to eligible public schools and public charter schools based on one or more of the
63 following criteria:
64 (a) Student academic growth above the state average, including value-added
65 growth and growth relative to grade level;
66 (b) Demonstrated improvement in student outcomes over multiple years;
67 (c) Significant improvement among historically underserved student groups;
68 (d) High levels of student readiness for postsecondary education, training, or
69 employment; or
70 (e) Demonstrated improvement in early literacy outcomes, including growth in
71 reading achievement for students in kindergarten through grade three, as measured by
72 multiple indicators of reading development and progress over time.
73 (3) The department shall establish, by rule, the methodology for determining
74 eligibility, award amounts, and distribution of funds under this subsection.
75 (4) No school or charter school shall be guaranteed funding under this
76 subsection, and awards shall be made only to the extent that funds are appropriated by
77 the general assembly.
78 (5) Moneys shall be distributed to the school's fiscal agent before December first
79 of each year, and one hundred percent of the moneys received shall be placed in the
80 school's classroom incidental fund to be used in a manner determined by the governing
81 body of the district or charter school to promote teacher recruitment and retention.
82 7. (1) The department shall develop a critera-referenced growth measure, called
83 "growth relative to grade level", in addition to the existing normative value-added
84 growth measure.
85 (2) Growth relative to grade level shall evaluate for each student with two
86 consecutive years of Missouri assessment program performance levels whether that
87 student has made sufficient progress to put them on a trajectory to reach or exceed
88 grade-level within three years or by the end of grade ten, whichever comes first.
89 8. The rating under subsection 4 of this section for a public school district, public
90 school, or public charter school shall be based on the following factors:

140 serve in national or community service, are employed in the farming or ranching

143 12. For public school districts, the annual school accountability report card
144 rating shall be calculated following the same method used for calculating a school report
145 except at the district level.
146 13. The state board of education shall provide a statewide report card that
147 provides the following:
148 (1) The percentages of Missouri students annually attending schools rated "A",
149 "B", "C", "D", or "F", with the objective of increasing the percentage of students
150 attending more highly rated schools each year; and
151 (2) The performance of Missouri students on state assessments relative to the
152 National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP), with the objective that
153 proficiency rates on both assessments be reasonably aligned.
154 14. For the purposes of this section, the development and publication of an
155 annual school accountability report card shall not be required for any special school
156 district or state-operated school in which all of the students enrolled are students with
157 disabilities.
