6102S.08C KRISTINA MARTIN, Secretary
AN ACT
To repeal section 162.821, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof three new sections relating to
accountability measures for elementary and secondary schools.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Section 162.821, RSMo, is repealed and three
2 new sections enacted in lieu thereof, to be known as sections
3 160.524, 162.192, and 162.821, to read as follows:
1 160.524. 1. (1) The state board of education shall
2 develop an annual school accountability report card for each
3 public school district, public school, and public charter
4 school in the state for the purpose of providing information
5 about school performance in accordance with the provisions
6 of this section. In addition to providing information about
7 student performance, the report card shall be designed to
8 satisfy federal reporting requirements for the disclosure of
9 information about students, staff, finances, and other data.
10 (2) Each school accountability report card shall be
11 designed in a standardized, clear, and easily accessible
12 form so that the accountability report card can be easily
13 understood by parents, taxpayers, school personnel,
14 legislators, and the media and shall include a summary
15 intended for parents and guardians that:

18 (b) Identifies areas of strength and areas in need of

21 student academic growth; and
22 (d) Provides information on how families may engage
23 with the school to support student learning.
24 The plain-language summary shall include a clear explanation
25 of student academic growth and how the school's growth
26 compares to the state's expectations.

28 duties of implementing the provisions of this section to the
29 department of elementary and secondary education or may
30 contract with a private entity in accordance with the

41 public school district, public school, and public charter
42 school shall publish in a clear and easily accessible
43 location on its website the district and school
44 accountability report cards including each underlying
45 indicator that is used to derive the performance rating and
46 the rates of pupil attendance from the previous school year.

47 4. The state board of education shall assign each
48 public school district, public school, and public charter
49 school one of the following ratings, which shall be based on
50 a zero-to-one-hundred scale:
51 (1) "A" for those districts and schools producing
52 excellent student outcomes;
53 (2) "B" for those districts and schools producing more-
54 than-satisfactory student outcomes;
55 (3) "C" for those districts and schools producing
56 satisfactory student outcomes;
57 (4) "D" for those districts and schools producing less-
58 than-satisfactory student outcomes; and
59 (5) "F" for those districts and schools failing to
60 produce adequate student outcomes.

62 public school district, public school, and public charter
63 school an overall performance rating of "A", "B", "C", "D",
64 or "F", derived from multiple measures of school performance
65 as provided in this section.
66 (2) The benchmark for excellent student outcomes or an
67 "A" rating shall be the top decile of the composite score of
68 the measures outlined in subsection 8 of this section.
69 (3) A public school district, public school, or public
70 charter school that does not test at least ninety-five
71 percent of its students shall have assessment participation
72 rates reported separately and clearly identified on the
73 school accountability report card. Assessment participation
74 rates shall not result in an automatic reduction of the
75 district's or school's performance level but shall be
76 included to provide transparency and context for parents and
77 the public.

78 6. (1) There is hereby created the "Show Me Success
79 Program" to provide additional funding, subject to
80 appropriation, to public schools and public charter schools
81 that demonstrate strong student academic growth, sustained
82 improvement over time, or success in closing achievement
83 gaps, as determined by the department of elementary and
84 secondary education.
85 (2) Subject to appropriations, the department may
86 award performance-based funding to eligible public schools
87 and public charter schools based on one or more of the
88 following criteria:
89 (a) Student academic growth above the state average,
90 including value-added growth and growth to proficiency;
91 (b) Demonstrated improvement in student outcomes over
92 multiple years;
93 (c) Significant improvement among historically
94 underserved student groups;
95 (d) High levels of student readiness for postsecondary
96 education, training, or employment; or
97 (e) Demonstrated improvement in early literacy
98 outcomes, including growth in reading achievement for
99 students in kindergarten through grade three, as measured by
100 multiple indicators of reading development and progress over
101 time.
102 (3) The department shall establish, by rule, the
103 methodology for determining eligibility, award amounts, and
104 distribution of funds under this subsection.
105 (4) No school or charter school shall be guaranteed
106 funding under this subsection, and awards shall be made only
107 to the extent that funds are appropriated by the general
108 assembly.

110 agent before December first of each year, and one hundred
111 percent of the moneys received shall be placed in the
112 school's classroom incidental fund to be used in a manner
113 determined by the governing body of the district or charter
114 school to promote teacher recruitment and retention.
115 7. (1) The department shall develop a criteria-
116 referenced growth measure, called "growth to proficiency",
117 in addition to the existing normative value-added growth
118 measure.

121 program performance levels whether that student has made
122 sufficient progress to put them on a trajectory to reach or
123 exceed grade-level within three years or by the end of grade
124 ten, whichever comes first.

131 the Missouri assessment program;
132 (2) Student academic growth in English language arts,
133 math, and science using a growth to proficiency metric and a
134 value-added growth metric of:
135 (a) All students; and
136 (b) The lowest-performing quartile of students; and
137 (3) For high schools:
138 (a) The student four-year graduation rate; and
139 (b) A "Success-Ready Graduate" measure, calculated by
140 the department of elementary and secondary education, which

144 achieve a score of three or higher;

147 which students achieve a score of four or higher;
148 c. Dual enrollment in core subjects with a "C" course
149 grade or higher;
150 d. Earning an industry-recognized credential or career
151 technical education certificate related to an occupation
152 that is high wage, high demand;
153 e. Completion of an early bachelor's or associate
154 degree; and
155 f. Completion of stackable credentials.

157 section for elementary and middle schools shall be based on
158 overall student academic achievement level, value-added
159 growth, and growth to proficiency as follows:
160 (1) Academic achievement level, value-added academic

166 percent of the rating;

170 percent of the rating.
