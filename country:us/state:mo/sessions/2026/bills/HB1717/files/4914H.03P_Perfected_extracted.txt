AN ACT
To repeal section 191.480, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof ten new sections relating to
alternative therapies.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Section 191.480, RSMo, is repealed and ten new sections enacted in lieu
2 thereof, to be known as sections 191.479, 191.480, 191.1610, 191.1613, 191.1616, 191.1619,
3 191.1622, 191.1625, 191.1628, and 630.1170, to read as follows:
191.479. 1. As used in this section, the following terms mean:
2 (1) "Bona fide physician-patient relationship", a relationship between a
3 physician and a patient in which the physician:
4 (a) Has completed an assessment of the patient's medical history and current
5 medical condition, including an in-person examination of the patient;
6 (b) Has consulted with the patient with respect to the patient's medical
7 condition; and
8 (c) Is available to provide follow-up care and treatment to the patient;
9 (2) "Facilitator", an individual who is present with a person who uses psilocybin
10 in order to facilitate the therapeutic use of the psilocybin for the person;
11 (3) "First responder", any police officer; firefighter; dispatcher for police, fire,
12 or emergency medical services purposes; emergency medical technician or responder;
13 ambulance or air ambulance operator; or emergency room physician or nurse;
14 (4) "Veteran", any person defined as a veteran by the United States Department
15 of Veterans Affairs or its successor agency.
