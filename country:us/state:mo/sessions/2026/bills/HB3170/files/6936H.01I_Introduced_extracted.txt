103RD GENERAL ASSEMBLY

6936H.01I JOSEPH ENGLER, Chief Clerk
AN ACT
To amend chapter 172, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to the powers of the
curators of the University of Missouri.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Chapter 172, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto one new section, to be
2 known as section 172.975, to read as follows:
172.975. 1. In exercising the powers granted under section 172.020 and other
2 powers granted by statute or the Constitution of Missouri, the curators of the University
3 of Missouri are hereby authorized and empowered to acquire, manage, lease, purchase,
4 sell, contract for, or otherwise acquire an interest in or participate in the ownership or
5 operation of hospital, medical, or other health care facilities or providers, including but
6 not limited to through the acquisition of entities engaged in the provision of health care
7 services or the acquisition of assets of such entities, regardless of the competitive
8 consequences of these activities.
9 2. The state finds that the benefits of the university engaging in the activities
10 described in this section include preserving and expanding access to needed health care
11 services; expanding training opportunities for physicians, nurses, therapists, and other
12 health care professionals; enhancing the ability of the university to advance research
13 and innovation in health care and other scientific and technological fields; furthering
14 economic development throughout the state by enhancing the quality of, and expanding
15 access to, health care delivered to medically underserved populations; managing costs;
16 and improving the efficiency of the health care services it delivers. The state finds that
17 these benefits outweigh any adverse impact on competition.
EXPLANATION — Matter enclosed in bold-faced brackets [thus] in the above bill is not enacted and is
intended to be omitted from the law. Matter in bold-face type in the above bill is proposed language.
