one new section, to be known as section 226.1250, to read as

Section 4:
226.1250. 1. This section shall be known and may be

Section 5:
2. As used in this section, the term "wrong-way
vehicle detection system" or "WWVDS" means a system
consisting of equipment for wrong-way vehicle detection,
wrong-way and do not enter signs enhanced with light-
emitting diodes or similar enhanced visibility features, and
real-time alerts.

Section 6:
3. The department of transportation shall implement
WWVDSs beginning no later than March 1, 2027. The
department shall prioritize the installation of a WWVDS at
the intersection of westbound Interstate 64 and Jefferson
Avenue. The implementation of other WWVDSs will be upon

Section 7:
4. The department shall evaluate the feasibility and
effectiveness of a system to notify other drivers in the
area about a wrong-way driver.

Section 8:
5. The department shall evaluate the feasibility and
effectiveness of a system to notify emergency responders of
a wrong-way driver.
1

Section 9:
6. Beginning December 31, 2027, and annually
thereafter, the department shall produce a report on the
implementation and effectiveness of WWVDSs, to include:
(1) The number and locations of systems currently
implemented;
(2) The number and locations of systems planned to be
implemented;
(3) The effect on wrong-way driving incidents and
fatalities;
(4) The cost-effectiveness of implemented systems; and
(5) Any adjustments to implementation strategies to be
made based on these or other factors.
