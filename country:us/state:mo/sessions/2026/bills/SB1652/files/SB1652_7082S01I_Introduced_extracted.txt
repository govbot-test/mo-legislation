103RD GENERA L ASSEMBLY

AN ACT
To amend chapter 595, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to the office of missing
and murdered African American women and girls.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Chapter 595, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto
2 one new section, to be known as section 595.326, to read as
3 follows:
1 595.326. 1. The director of the department of public
2 safety shall establish and maintain an office dedicated to
3 preventing and ending the targeting of African American
4 women and girls within the Missouri department of public
5 safety.
6 2. (1) The director shall appoint an executive
7 director for the office who is a person closely connected to
8 the African American community and who is highly
9 knowledgeable about criminal investigations. The director
10 is encouraged to consider candidates for appointment who are
11 recommended by members of the African American community.
12 (2) The executive director may select, appoint, and
13 compensate out of available funds assistants and employees
14 as necessary to discharge the office's responsibilities.
15 3. (1) The office has the following duties:

145 5. The office shall report on measurable outcomes
146 achieved to meet its statutory duties, along with specific
147 objectives and outcome measures proposed for the following
148 year. The report shall include data and statistics on
149 missing and murdered African American women and girls in
150 Missouri, including names, dates of disappearance, and dates
151 of death, to the extent the data is publicly available. The
152 office shall submit the report by January fifteenth each
153 year to the chairs and ranking minority members of the
154 legislative committees with primary jurisdiction over public
155 safety.
156 6. There is hereby created in the state treasury the
157 "Missing and Murdered African American Women and Girls
158 Fund", which shall consist of moneys appropriated by the
159 general assembly, gifts, and grants. The state treasurer
160 shall be custodian of the fund. In accordance with sections
161 30.170 and 30.180, the state treasurer may approve
162 disbursements. The fund shall be a dedicated fund and money
163 in the fund shall be used solely by the director of public
164 safety for the purposes authorized by this section.
165 Notwithstanding the provisions of section 33.080 to the
166 contrary, any moneys remaining in the fund at the end of the
167 biennium shall not revert to the credit of the general
168 revenue fund. The state treasurer shall invest moneys in
169 the fund in the same manner as other funds are invested.
170 Any interest and moneys earned on such investments shall be
171 credited to the fund.

175 girls, or to provide assistance to victims of offenses that
