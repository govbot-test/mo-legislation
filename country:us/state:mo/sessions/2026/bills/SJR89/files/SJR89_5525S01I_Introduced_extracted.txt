1 Section 4(b). 1. Property in classes 1 and 2 and

4 be fixed by law for each class and for each subclass.

11 (1) Residential property;
12 (2) Agricultural and horticultural property;
13 (3) Utility, industrial, commercial, railroad, and all
14 other property not included in subclasses (1) and (2) of
15 class 1.
16 Property in the subclasses of class 1 may be defined by law,
17 however subclasses (1), (2), and (3) shall not be further
18 divided, provided, land in subclass (2) may by general law
19 be assessed for tax purposes on its productive capability.
20 The same percentage of value shall be applied to all

23 of thirty-three and one-third percent.

25 of this section and section 3 of this article to the
26 contrary, beginning January 1, 2027, for all property in
27 subclass (1) of class 1 that has been maintained by the

29 such property shall be deemed to be the same value
30 determined at the most recent previous assessment of the
31 property.
32 (2) In a reassessment of property in subclass (1) of
33 class 1 that has been maintained by the homeowner as his or
34 her primary residence, the assessed valuation of such
35 property shall not be increased from the assessed valuation
36 of such property determined at its most recent previous
37 assessment while the owner or owners continue to own such
38 property. Real property in subclass (1) of class 1 that is
39 purchased, newly constructed, or undergoes a change in
40 ownership subsequent to the first assessment immediately
41 following the effective date of this section shall be

43 after which the assessed value of such property shall not
44 increase while the new owner or owners continue to own such
45 property.
46 (3) Notwithstanding subdivision (2) of this subsection
47 to the contrary, the assessed valuation of property in
