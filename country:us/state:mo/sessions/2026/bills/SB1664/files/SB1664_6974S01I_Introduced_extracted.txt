SECOND REGULAR SESSION
SENATE BILL NO. 1664
103RD GENERA L ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY SENATOR COLEMAN.
6974S.01I KRISTINA MARTIN, Secretary
AN ACT
To amend chapter 191, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to payment for health
care services, with penalty provisions.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:
1 Section A. Chapter 191, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto
2 one new section, to be known as section 191.2299, to read as
3 follows:
1 191.2299. 1. This section shall be known and may be
2 cited as the "Honest Billing Act".
3 2. As used in this section, the following terms shall
4 mean:
5 (1) "Campus", the same meaning as in 42 CFR 413.65(a),
6 as may be amended from time to time;
7 (2) "Enrollee", the same meaning as is ascribed to
8 such term in section 376.1350;
9 (3) "Facility", the same meaning as is ascribed to
10 such term in section 376.1350;
11 (4) "Health care service", the same meaning as is
12 ascribed to such term in section 376.1350;
13 (5) "Health carrier" or "carrier", the same meaning as
14 is ascribed to such term in section 376.1350;
15 (6) "National provider identifier" or "NPI", the
16 standard, unique health identifier for health care providers

SB 1664 2
17 that is issued by the National Plan and Provider Enumeration
18 System in accordance with 45 CFR 162;
19 (7) "Off-campus outpatient department of a facility",
20 a location:
21 (a) Whose operations are directly or indirectly owned
22 or controlled by, in whole or in part, or affiliated with, a
23 hospital, regardless of whether the operations are under the
24 same governing body as the hospital;
25 (b) That is located more than two hundred fifty yards
26 from the hospital's main campus;
27 (c) That provides services that are organizationally
28 and functionally integrated with the hospital; and
29 (d) That is an outpatient facility providing
30 preventive, diagnostic, treatment, or emergency services.
31 3. Except as otherwise specified in this section, this
32 section shall apply to all facilities licensed and operating
33 in this state and to all health carriers doing business in
34 this state. This section shall apply to claims submitted
35 after December 31, 2026.
36 4. Irrespective of 45 CFR 162.410(a)(1), each off-
37 campus outpatient department of a facility shall apply for,
38 obtain, and use, on all claims filed after the date
39 specified in subsection 3 of this section, for reimbursement
40 or payment for health care services provided in that
41 department, a unique NPI that is distinct from the NPI used
42 by the main campus of the facility and any other off-campus
43 location of the facility.
44 5. (1) No facility, or entity on behalf of a
45 facility, shall, with respect to health care services
46 furnished to an enrollee at an off-campus outpatient
47 department of a facility, submit a claim for such health
48 care services to a health carrier, or hold the enrollee

SB 1664 3
49 liable for such health care services, unless those health
50 care services are billed using the separate unique NPI
51 established for the off-campus outpatient department and on
52 a CMS 1500 form or a HIPAA X12 837P electronic claims
53 transaction or a successor form or transaction.
54 (2) No health carrier shall be responsible to
55 reimburse claims for health care services furnished to an
56 enrollee at an off-campus outpatient department of a
57 facility if such claims are not billed in accordance with
58 this subsection.
59 6. A facility, or entity on behalf of a facility, that
60 does not bill for health care services rendered to an
61 enrollee at an off-campus outpatient department of a
62 facility in accordance with this section shall not hold the
63 enrollee liable to pay for the health care services. A
64 violation of this subsection shall constitute a violation of
65 the Missouri merchandising practices act, sections 407.010
66 to 407.130, subject to enforcement by the attorney general.
67 7. A facility applying for a license or license
68 renewal by the state shall demonstrate that it has obtained
69 one or more NPIs as required under this section as a
70 condition of receiving licensure, and shall use its unique
71 NPI or NPIs on every claim for payment in the manner
72 required under this section.
73 8. The department of health and senior services may,
74 in accordance with chapter 536, impose penalties on any
75 licensee violating any of the provisions of this section by
76 taking any or all of the following actions, separately or in
77 combination:
78 (1) Imposing an administrative fine of one thousand
79 dollars for each violation of this section;

SB 1664 4
80 (2) Recovering reasonable investigative fees and costs
81 incurred as a consequence of the violation or violations;
82 (3) Suspending, revoking, or denying the issuance or
83 renewal of a license;
84 (4) Placing conditions on a license;
85 (5) Placing a licensee on probation;
86 (6) Referring a licensee to the attorney general for
87 investigation; and
88 (7) Requiring the provider to post information about
89 these penalties on the main page of its website.
90 9. The director of the department of commerce and
91 insurance shall have authority to refer any violation of
92 this section to the department of health and senior
93 services. The attorney general shall have authority to
94 enforce the provisions of this section.
95 10. The director of the department of commerce and
96 insurance may promulgate rules as necessary for the
97 implementation of this section. Any rule or portion of a
98 rule, as that term is defined in section 536.010, that is
99 created under the authority delegated in this section shall
100 become effective only if it complies with and is subject to
101 all of the provisions of chapter 536 and, if applicable,
102 section 536.028. This section and chapter 536 are
103 nonseverable and if any of the powers vested with the
104 general assembly pursuant to chapter 536 to review, to delay
105 the effective date, or to disapprove and annul a rule are
106 subsequently held unconstitutional, then the grant of
107 rulemaking authority and any rule proposed or adopted after
108 August 28, 2026, shall be invalid and void.
