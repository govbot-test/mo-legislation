10 506.400. 1. As used in this section, "claimant" means a person convicted and
11 subsequently imprisoned for one or more offenses that such person did not commit.
12 2. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, a claimant may bring an action in the
13 circuit court seeking damages from the state under this section.
14 3. (1) The claimant shall establish the following by a preponderance of evidence:
15 (a) The claimant was convicted of a felony offense and subsequently imprisoned;
16 (b) The claimant's judgment of conviction was reversed or vacated and either the charges
17 were dismissed or on retrial the claimant was found to be not guilty;
18 (c) The claimant did not commit the offense or offenses for which the claimant was
19 convicted and was not an accessory or accomplice to the acts that were the basis of the
20 conviction and resulted in a reversal or vacation of the judgment of conviction, dismissal of the
21 charges, or finding of not guilty on retrial; and
22 (d) The claimant did not commit or suborn perjury, fabricate evidence, or by the
23 claimant's own conduct cause or bring about the conviction. Neither a confession or admission
24 later found to be false nor a guilty plea shall constitute committing or suborning perjury,
25 fabricating evidence, or causing or bringing about the conviction under this subsection.
26 (2) The court, in exercising its discretion as permitted by law regarding the weight and
27 admissibility of evidence submitted under this section, may, in the interest of justice, give due
28 consideration to difficulties of proof caused by the passage of time, the death or unavailability of
29 witnesses, the destruction of evidence, or other factors not caused by such persons or those
30 acting on their behalf.
31 4. (1) The suit, accompanied by a statement of the facts concerning the claim for
32 damages, verified in the manner provided for the verification of complaints in the rules of civil
33 procedure, shall be brought by the claimant within a period of two years after the:
34 (a) Dismissal of the criminal charges against the claimant or finding of not guilty on
35 retrial; or
36 (b) Grant of a pardon to the claimant.
37 (2) A claimant convicted, imprisoned, and released from custody before August 28,
38 2026, shall commence an action under this section no later than August 28, 2028.
