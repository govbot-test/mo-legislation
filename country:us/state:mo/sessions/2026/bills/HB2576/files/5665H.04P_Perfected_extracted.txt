HOUSE SUBSTITUTE FOR
103RD GENERAL ASSEMBLY
5665H.04P JOSEPH ENGLER, Chief Clerk
AN ACT
To amend chapters 9, 10, 227, and 620, RSMo, by adding thereto thirty-five new sections
relating to state designations.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:

Section 2:
Section A. Chapters 9, 10, 227, and 620, RSMo, are amended by adding thereto
2 thirty-five new sections, to be known as sections 9.025, 9.358, 9.413, 9.418, 9.501, 9.502,
3 9.504, 9.509, 9.510, 9.516, 10.281, 10.282, 10.283, 10.284, 10.287, 10.288, 10.289, 10.292,
4 227.895, 227.898, 227.900, 227.901, 227.902, 227.903, 227.904, 227.905, 227.906, 227.907,
5 227.908, 227.909, 227.910, 227.911, 227.912, 227.913, and 620.430, to read as follows:

Section 3:
9.025. The month of January is hereby designated as "Blood Donor Awareness
2 Month". It is recommended to the people of this state that the month be appropriately
3 observed through activities that increase awareness of the importance of blood
4 donation. The state deems blood and blood products to be a critical resource and a vital
5 public health asset that must be readily available at all times.

Section 4:
9.358. April twenty-second each year is hereby designated as "Missouri Black
2 Bear Awareness Day". Citizens of this state are encouraged to participate in
3 appropriate events and activities to provide education about efforts to conserve
4 Missouri's black bear population.

Section 5:
9.413. November twenty-fourth each year is hereby designated as "Frankie
2 Muse Freeman Day" in Missouri. The citizens of this state are encouraged to
3 participate in appropriate events and activities to celebrate Frankie Muse Freeman, a
4 St. Louis attorney who was a champion for civil rights, won a landmark case that ended
5 legal racial discrimination in St. Louis public housing, and was the first black woman

6 appointed to the United States Commission on Civil Rights by president Lyndon B.
7 Johnson.

Section 6:
9.418. The last full week of April each year shall be known as "Infertility
2 Awareness Week" in Missouri. It is estimated that approximately one in eight couples in
3 the United States experience infertility, impacting people across all racial, ethnic,
4 socioeconomic, and cultural backgrounds. The citizens of this state are encouraged to
5 participate in appropriate events and activities to raise awareness about infertility.

Section 7:
9.501. The first full week in September each year is hereby designated as "June's
2 Week" and "Rare Pediatric Disease Week" in Missouri in honor of June, a young
3 Missourian undergoing treatment for an atypical teratoid rhabdoid tumor, subtype B
4 (ATRT-B). The citizens of this state are encouraged to participate in appropriate events
5 and activities to raise public awareness of rare pediatric diseases, encourage early
6 recognition of symptoms, and honor Missouri children and families affected by rare
7 pediatric diseases.

Section 8:
9.502. March twenty-sixth of each year is hereby designated as "Pediatric Acute-
2 Onset Neuropsychiatric Syndrome (PANS)/Pediatric Autoimmune Neuropsychiatric
3 Disorder Associated with Streptococcus (PANDAS) Awareness Day". The citizens of
4 this state are encouraged to participate in appropriate events and activities to raise
5 PANS/PANDAS awareness.

Section 9:
9.504. April fifth each year is hereby designated as "Racquetball Day" in
2 Missouri, in honor of Kirkwood Racquetball, USA Racquetball's five-time consecutive
3 high school national champions. The citizens of this state are encouraged to participate
4 in appropriate events and activities to learn more about the sport of racquetball and
5 celebrate Missouri's racquetball accomplishments.

Section 10:
9.509. April thirtieth each year is hereby designated as "William Lacy Clay Sr.
2 Day" in Missouri. The citizens of this state are encouraged to participate in appropriate
3 events and activities to celebrate the life and legacy of William Lacy Clay Sr., who
4 represented Missouri's first district in Congress from 1969 to 2001 and was one of the
5 founding members of the Congressional Black Caucus.

Section 11:
9.510. May tenth each year is hereby designated as "Missouri River Runner
2 Day" in Missouri. The citizens of this state are encouraged to participate in appropriate
3 events and activities to recognize the role of passenger rail in connecting Missouri
4 communities, supporting economic development, and promoting safe and reliable
5 transportation across the state.

Section 12:
9.516. June twenty-eighth each year is hereby designated as "Eliot and Muriel
2 Battle Day" in Missouri. The citizens of this state are encouraged to participate in
3 appropriate events and activities to celebrate the lives of Eliot Battle, the first Black
HS HB 2576 3
4 faculty member at Hickman High School in Columbia, and Muriel Battle, the first Black
5 principal in the Columbia Public School District. The couple worked as a team to ease
6 racial tensions during the 1960s and have been recognized with schools in Columbia
7 named in their honor: Eliot Battle Elementary School and Muriel Williams Battle High
8 School.

Section 13:
10.281. The smooth chanterelle, scientifically known as Cantharellus lateritius, is
2 selected for and shall be known as the official mushroom for the state of Missouri.

Section 14:
10.282. The city of Dexter is selected for and shall be known as the official "Rib
2 City" for the state of Missouri.

Section 15:
10.283. Missouri Military Academy in the city of Mexico is selected for and shall
2 be known as the official military academy of the state of Missouri.

Section 16:
10.284. The Missouri Cherry Blossom Festival in the city of Marshfield is
2 selected for and shall be known as the official state cherry blossom festival for the state
3 of Missouri.

Section 17:
10.287. The city of Kansas City is selected for and shall be known as the official
2 "Barbecue Capital" of the state of Missouri.

Section 18:
10.288. The city of Belle is selected for and shall be known as the official
2 "Cowbell Capital" of the state of Missouri.

Section 19:
10.289. The city of Bland is selected for and shall be officially known as "The
2 Silver City of the Rock Island Trail" for the state of Missouri.

Section 20:
10.292. The Kansas City Chiefs shall not be the official professional football
2 team of the state of Missouri.

Section 21:
227.895. The portion of State Highway D from State Highway C continuing
2 north to State Highway Y in St. Francois County shall be designated as the "Carl G
3 Koester Memorial Highway". The department of transportation shall erect and
4 maintain signs designating such highway, with the costs to be paid by private donations.

Section 22:
227.898. The portion of State Highway WW from State Highway 13 continuing
2 to State Highway H in Greene County shall be designated the "Gary D McCormack
3 Nemo Macto Unus Memorial Highway". The department of transportation shall erect
4 and maintain appropriate signs designating such highway, with the costs to be paid by
5 the department of transportation.

Section 23:
227.900. The bridge on CST Dougherty Ferry Road crossing over Interstate 270
2 in St. Louis County shall be designated the "Army SSG Salvatore Palazzolo Memorial
3 Bridge". The department of transportation shall erect and maintain appropriate signs
4 designating such bridge, with the costs to be paid by the department of transportation.

2 Ferguson Spring Road in Jackson County shall be designated the "WWII Robert Earl

2 361 in Johnson County shall be designated the "Captain Vernon Collett Memorial
3 Highway". The department of transportation shall erect and maintain appropriate
4 signs designating such highway, with the costs to be paid by the department of
5 transportation.

Section 26:
227.903. The bridge on State Highway CC crossing over Interstate 44 in Webster
2 County shall be designated the "Dr Tommy Macdonnell Memorial Bridge". The
3 department of transportation shall erect and maintain appropriate signs designating
4 such bridge. Notwithstanding provisions of section 227.296, the costs shall be paid by
5 the department of transportation in recognition of the honoree's Silver Star Award.

Section 27:
227.904. The portion of State Highway 13 from Interstate 35 continuing north to
2 U.S. Highway 136/69 in Harrison County shall be designated the "Dr Scott Hall

227.905. The bridge on Business 63 crossing over U.S. Highway 60 in Howell
2 County shall be designated the "Jerrad Bennett Memorial Bridge". The department of
3 transportation shall erect and maintain appropriate signs designating such bridge, with
4 the costs to be paid by private donations.

227.906. The portion of State Highway 9 from Horizons Parkway continuing
2 north to Park College Entrance Drive in Platte County shall be designated as the
3 "Firefighter Paramedic Graham J Hoffman Memorial Highway". The department of
4 transportation shall erect and maintain appropriate signs designating such highway,
5 with the costs to be paid by the department of transportation.

Section 30:
227.907. The bridge on State Highway 139 crossing over Barber Creek in
2 Putnam County shall be designated the "PVT William W Smith Memorial Bridge".

227.908. The bridge on State Highway 139 crossing over Hooton/Newtown Creek
2 in Sullivan County shall be designated the "CPL Vernon D Jobe Memorial Bridge".
3 The department of transportation shall erect and maintain appropriate signs

Section 37:
620.430. 1. There is hereby established the designation of "Missouri Rock Island
2 Trail Community" for municipalities located along the Rock Island Trail corridor that
3 promote outdoor recreation, heritage tourism, and economic development within this
4 state.
5 2. The following municipalities are hereby designated as Missouri Rock Island
6 Trail communities:
7 (1) The City of Bland; and
8 (2) The City of Belle.
9 3. Additional municipalities located along the Rock Island Trail corridor may be
10 designated as Missouri Rock Island Trail Communities by act of the general assembly.
11 4. Nothing in this section shall be construed to require the expenditure of state
12 funds.
