1

7 the most common form of dementia for people under the age of

9 participate in appropriate events and activities that will
10 increase awareness of frontotemporal degeneration, to
11 support programs of research and education surrounding

13 families impacted by frontotemporal degeneration.

1 9.405. April twenty-seventh each year is hereby
2 designated as "Ulysses S. Grant Day" in Missouri. The
3 citizens of this state are encouraged to participate in
4 appropriate events and activities to celebrate the life of
5 the Civil War general and eighteenth president of the United
6 States, who made his home in St. Louis, Missouri, in the

1 9.412. The month of September each year is hereby
2 designated as "Brain Aneurysm Awareness Month" in Missouri.
3 The citizens of this state are encouraged to participate in
4 appropriate events and activities to raise awareness about
5 the causes of and treatments for brain aneurysms, which
6 affect nearly two hundred thousand people each year.
1 9.413. November twenty-fourth each year is hereby
2 designated as "Frankie Muse Freeman Day" in Missouri. The
3 citizens of this state are encouraged to participate in
4 appropriate events and activities to celebrate Frankie Muse
5 Freeman, a St. Louis attorney who was a champion for civil
6 rights, won a landmark case that ended legal racial
7 discrimination in St. Louis public housing, and was the
8 first black woman appointed to the United States Commission
9 on Civil Rights by president Lyndon B. Johnson.
1 9.418. The last full week of April each year shall be
2 known as "Infertility Awareness Week" in Missouri. It is
3 estimated that approximately one in eight couples in the
4 United States experience infertility, impacting people
5 across all racial, ethnic, socioeconomic, and cultural
3

6 backgrounds. The citizens of this state are encouraged to
7 participate in appropriate events and activities to raise
8 awareness about infertility.
1 9.501. The first full week in September each year is
2 hereby designated as "June's Week" and "Rare Pediatric
3 Disease Week" in Missouri in honor of June, a young
4 Missourian undergoing treatment for an atypical teratoid
5 rhabdoid tumor, subtype B (ATRT-B). The citizens of this
6 state are encouraged to participate in appropriate events
7 and activities to raise public awareness of rare pediatric
8 diseases, encourage early recognition of symptoms, and honor
9 Missouri children and families affected by rare pediatric
10 diseases.

6 participate in appropriate events and activities to raise
7 PANS/PANDAS awareness.

4

1 9.510. May tenth each year is hereby designated as
2 "Missouri River Runner Day" in Missouri. The citizens of

4 events and activities to recognize the role of passenger

1 9.516. June twenty-eighth each year is hereby
2 designated as "Eliot and Muriel Battle Day" in Missouri.
3 The citizens of this state are encouraged to participate in
4 appropriate events and activities to celebrate the lives of
5 Eliot Battle, the first Black faculty member at Hickman High
6 School in Columbia, and Muriel Battle, the first Black
7 principal in the Columbia Public School District. The
8 couple worked as a team to ease racial tensions during the
9 1960s and have been recognized with schools in Columbia
10 named in their honor: Eliot Battle Elementary School and
11 Muriel Williams Battle High School.
