Section 1:
4711H02.10H
HB 2355
Offered By
_____________________________________ ___________________________________
1 AMEND House Committee Substitute for House Bill No. 2355, Page 1, Section 208.270, Lines
2 4-15, by deleting said lines and inserting in lieu thereof the following:
3
4 "(1) "Clinically indicated vitamin therapy", vitamin supplementation prescribed to treat a
5 diagnosed micronutrient deficiency supported by laboratory evidence, or prescribed for a
6 guideline supported high-risk clinical condition for which supplementation is medically
7 necessary. Vitamins used solely for general wellness or without a disease directed indication
8 shall not be covered, consistent with federal medical expense standards;
9 (2) "Medically tailored groceries", a selection of groceries, including but not limited to
10 fruits, vegetables, grains, beans, lean proteins, or dairy, that is prescribed by a registered dietitian
11 or qualified clinical team for individuals who are able to prepare food. Such groceries may be
12 specifically selected by a registered dietitian or qualified clinical team, or selected by a
13 participant from a pantry in accordance with dietary recommendations set by a registered
14 dietitian or qualified clinical team;
15 (3) "Medically tailored meals", meals used to meet the specific dietary needs of
16 individuals living with one or more chronic conditions or diet-related conditions. Such meals are
17 prescribed by a registered dietitian or qualified clinical team and home-delivered for individuals
18 who are not able to prepare food on their own;
19 (4) "Prescription micronutrient therapy", the provision of vitamins or single-nutrient
20 supplements used primarily for the diagnosis, cure, mitigation, treatment, or prevention of
21 disease, and not primarily for general health or wellness, consistent with the federal definition of
22 "medical care";
23 (5) "Produce prescriptions", the prescription of fruits, vegetables, or other healthy foods
24 with no added fats, sugars, or salts to at-risk patients by a registered dietitian or qualified clinical
25 team, typically in the form of coupons or vouchers for local farmers' markets, grocery stores, or
26 mobile markets."; and
27
28 Further amend said bill and section, Page 2, Lines 30-31, by deleting said lines and inserting in
29 lieu thereof the following:
30
31 "(b) Medically tailored meals;
32 (c) Produce prescriptions; and
33 (d) Prescription micronutrient therapy, including coverage of vitamins when clinically
34 indicated."; and
35
36 Further amend said bill and section, Page 2, Line 43, by deleting the number "2025" and
