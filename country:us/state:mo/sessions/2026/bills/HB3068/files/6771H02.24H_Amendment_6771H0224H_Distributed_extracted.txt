4 "632.305. 1. An application for detention for evaluation and treatment at a mental health
5 facility may be executed by any adult person, who need not be an attorney or represented by an
6 attorney, on a form provided by the court for such purpose, and shall allege under oath[, without
7 a notarization requirement,] that the applicant has reason to believe that the respondent is
8 suffering from a mental disorder and presents a likelihood of serious harm to himself or herself
9 or to others. The application shall specify the factual information on which such belief is based
10 and should contain the names and addresses of all persons known to the applicant who have
11 knowledge of such facts through personal observation.
12 2. The filing of a written application in court by any adult person, who need not be an
13 attorney or represented by an attorney, shall authorize the applicant to bring the matter before the
14 court on an ex parte basis to determine whether the respondent should be taken into custody and
15 transported to a mental health facility. The application may be filed in the court having probate
16 jurisdiction in any county where the respondent may be found. If the court finds that there is
17 probable cause, either upon testimony under oath or upon a review of affidavits, declarations, or
18 other supporting documentation, to believe that the respondent may be suffering from a mental
19 disorder and presents a likelihood of serious harm to himself or herself or others, it shall direct a
20 peace officer to take the respondent into custody and transport him or her to a mental health
21 facility for detention for evaluation and treatment for a period not to exceed ninety-six hours
22 unless further detention and treatment is authorized pursuant to this chapter. Nothing herein
23 shall be construed to prohibit the court, in the exercise of its discretion, from giving the
24 respondent an opportunity to be heard.
25 3. A peace officer may take a person into custody for detention for evaluation and
26 treatment at a mental health facility for a period not to exceed ninety-six hours only when such
27 peace officer has reasonable cause to believe that such person is suffering from a mental disorder
28 and that the likelihood of serious harm by such person to himself or herself or others is imminent
29 unless such person is immediately taken into custody. Upon arrival at the mental health facility,
30 the peace officer who conveyed such person or caused him or her to be conveyed shall either
