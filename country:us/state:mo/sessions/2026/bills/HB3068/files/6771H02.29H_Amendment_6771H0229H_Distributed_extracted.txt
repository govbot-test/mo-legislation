4 "644.800. 1. As used in this section, the following terms mean:
5 (1) "Department", the department of natural resources;
6 (2) "Discharge", a release by any means including, but not limited to, spilling, leaking,
7 pumping, pouring, spraying, emitting, disposing, escaping, emptying, or dumping, whether
8 intentional or unintentional;
9 (3) "Perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances" or "PFAS", any perfluoroalkyl and
10 polyfluoroalkyl substances listed in the toxic chemical list under section 313 of the Emergency
11 Planning and Community Right-to-Know Act (EPCRA), as amended;
12 (4) "Person", the same as defined under section 644.016;
13 (5) "Waters of the state", the same as defined under section 644.016.
14 2. The department shall promulgate rules regulating the manufacture, use, storage, and
15 remediation of firefighting or fire-suppressing foam to which PFAS have been intentionally
16 added. Such rules and regulations shall be designed to protect the environment and ensure safe
17 and clean soil, water, and air for the state of Missouri.
18 3. A person that discharges or causes to be discharged firefighting or fire-suppressing
19 foam to which PFAS have been intentionally added into or upon any lake, pond, river, stream,
20 sewer, surface water drainage, groundwater, other waters of the state, or any public or private
21 water supply or onto land adjacent to, on, or over such waters shall report the discharge to the
22 department as soon as practicable, but no later than twenty-four hours after the discharge occurs.
23 4. Beginning January 1, 2028, a person shall not manufacture, sell, offer for sale,
24 distribute for sale, or distribute for use in this state a firefighting or fire-suppressing foam to
25 which PFAS have been intentionally added, except in the following circumstances:
26 (1) Such foam is manufactured, sold, or distributed for use at an airport in the state, so
27 long as the foam is required by federal law or regulation to be used at airports for firefighting or
28 fire-suppressing purposes including, but not limited to, as required by 14 CFR Part 139. The
29 exception in this subdivision shall not apply if such foam is not required by federal law or
30 regulation; or
