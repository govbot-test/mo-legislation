103RD GENERAL ASSEMBLY

AN ACT
To repeal section 557.035, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof two new sections relating to the
offense of masked intimidation, with penalty provisions.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Section 557.035, RSMo, is repealed and two new sections enacted in lieu
2 thereof, to be known as sections 557.035 and 565.097, to read as follows:
557.035. 1. For all violations of section 565.054 or 565.090, subdivision (1) of
2 subsection 1 of section 569.100, or subdivision (1), (2), (3), (4), (6), (7) or (8) of subsection 1
3 of section 571.030, which the state believes to be knowingly motivated because of race, color,
4 religion, national origin, sex, sexual orientation or disability of the victim or victims, the state
5 may charge the offense or offenses under this section, and the violation is a class D felony.
6 2. For all violations of section 565.056; subdivision (1) of subsection 1 of section
7 569.090; section 565.097; subdivision (1) of subsection 1 of section 569.120; section
8 569.140; or section 574.050; which the state believes to be knowingly motivated because of
9 race, color, religion, national origin, sex, sexual orientation or disability of the victim or
10 victims, the state may charge the offense or offenses under this section, and the violation is a
11 class E felony.
12 3. The court shall assess punishment in all of the cases in which the state pleads and
13 proves any of the motivating factors listed in this section.
565.097. 1. A person commits the offense of masked intimidation if the person
2 intentionally harasses, intimidates, or threatens any other person while hiding or
3 concealing his or her face with a mask, hood, or any other article or device for the

4 purpose of concealing his or her identity and with the intent to place another person in
5 reasonable fear for his or her physical safety.
6 2. The offense of masked intimidation is a class C misdemeanor, unless it is a
7 second or subsequent offense of this section, in which case the offense is a class B
8 misdemeanor.
9 3. This section shall not apply to any person wearing a mask or otherwise
10 covering one's face for any purpose other than a purpose specified in subsection 1 of this
11 section, in cases where a person is wearing a:
12 (1) Mask or face covering on or near the occasion of a holiday, celebration, or
13 other event involving costumes;
14 (2) Mask, hood, article, or other device for the purpose of ensuring the physical
15 safety of the wearer or because of the nature of the person's occupation, trade, or
16 profession;
17 (3) Mask, hood, article, or other device for the purpose of protection from the
18 weather elements or while participating in a winter sport;
19 (4) Mask, hood, article, or other device in an artistic or theatrical production or
20 celebration;
21 (5) Gas mask or other protective facial covering for the purposes of protection
22 during or related to emergency situations or during emergency management drills;
23 (6) A mask for the purpose of ensuring one's physical health and safety or the
24 health and safety of others, including but not limited to limiting the spread of airborne
25 illnesses; or
26 (7) Any garb for religious purposes.
27 4. Nothing in this section shall be construed to diminish or infringe upon any
28 right protected under the First Amendment to the Constitution of the United States.
29 5. For purposes of this section, the following terms mean:
30 (1) "Harass", engaging in a knowing and willful pattern of conduct directed at a
31 particular person or particular group of persons that is intended to cause that person or
32 that group of persons to reasonably fear for their safety or suffer substantial emotional
33 distress;
34 (2) "Intimidate", willfully and substantially interfering, by threats, intimidation,
35 or coercion, with the exercise or enjoyment by any other person of rights secured by the
36 constitution or laws of the United States, or of rights secured by the constitution or laws
37 of this state, because of that person or person's actual or perceived race, color, religion,
38 national origin, ethnicity, sex, gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation,
39 or disability;
