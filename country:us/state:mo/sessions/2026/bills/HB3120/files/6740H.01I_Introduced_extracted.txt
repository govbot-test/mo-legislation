SECOND REGULAR SESSION
HOUSE BILL NO. 3120
103RD GENERAL ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY REPRESENTATIVE WHALEY.
6740H.01I JOSEPH ENGLER, Chief Clerk
AN ACT
To repeal section 110.240, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof one new section relating to
county treasurers.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Section 110.240, RSMo, is repealed and one new section enacted in lieu
2 thereof, to be known as section 110.240, to read as follows:
110.240. 1. It is the duty of the county treasurer to draw a check as county treasurer
2 upon a county depositary in favor of the legal holder thereof, and to charge the same to the
3 fund upon which it is drawn. No county treasurer shall draw any check upon the funds in any
4 depositary unless there is sufficient money belonging to the fund upon which the check is
5 drawn to pay the same, and no money belonging to the county shall be paid by any depositary
6 except upon checks of the county treasurer. In case any bonds, coupons or other indebtedness
7 of the county are payable by the terms of the bonds, coupons or other debts at any particular
8 place other than the treasury of the county, nothing contained in this section shall prevent any
9 county commission from causing the treasurer to place a sufficient sum at the place where
10 such debts shall be payable, at the time of their maturity, to meet the same.
11 2. As provided in this section and in sections 50.166 and 54.140, the county
12 treasurer’s signature shall be the only signature authorized or permitted on any bank
13 signature card, deposit agreement, or other authorization document for any account
14 maintained in a county depositary for county funds under the treasurer’s custody. No
15 other county official or employee shall be authorized or required to be listed as a signer
16 on any such account. Each county depositary shall ensure compliance with this
17 subsection.
EXPLANATION — Matter enclosed in bold-faced brackets [thus] in the above bill is not enacted and is
intended to be omitted from the law. Matter in bold-face type in the above bill is proposed language.
