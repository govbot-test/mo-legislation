SECOND REGULAR SESSION
HOUSE BILL NO. 1939
103RD GENERAL ASSEMBLY
INTRODUCED BY REPRESENTATIVE MURPHY.
4472H.01I JOSEPH ENGLER, Chief Clerk
AN ACT
To repeal section 304.034, RSMo, and to enact in lieu thereof one new section relating to golf
cart operation.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Section 304.034, RSMo, is repealed and one new section enacted in lieu
2 thereof, to be known as section 304.034, to read as follows:
304.034. 1. Notwithstanding any other law to the contrary, the governing body of any
2 municipality may by resolution or ordinance allow persons to operate golf carts or motorized
3 wheelchairs upon any street or highway under the governing body's jurisdiction that has a
4 posted speed limit of no more than twenty-five miles per hour. A golf cart or motorized
5 wheelchair shall not be operated at any time on any state or federal highway, but may be
6 operated upon such highway in order to cross a portion of the state highway system which
7 intersects a municipal street. No golf cart or motorized wheelchair shall cross any highway at
8 an intersection where the highway being crossed has a posted speed limit of more than forty-
9 five miles per hour.
10 2. Golf carts operating on city streets under the provisions of this section shall be
11 equipped with the following:
12 (1) At least two headlamps mounted at the same level with at least one on each
13 side of the front of the vehicle;
14 (2) At least two tail lamps;
15 (3) At least two rear lamps that when lighted will exhibit a red light plainly
16 visible from a distance of fifty feet to the rear;
17 (4) At least electrical or mechanical turn signal devices;

20 (6) At least one red reflector on the rear of the vehicle;
21 (7) One exterior mirror mounted on the driver's side of the vehicle;

32 3. In addition to safety equipment required under subsection 2 of this section,
33 golf carts operated on city streets [shall be equipped with adequate brakes and] shall meet any
34 other safety requirements imposed by the governing body.
35 4. Golf carts are not subject to the registration provisions of chapter 301.
36 [3.] 5. A person operating a golf cart under this section shall have a valid driver's
37 license issued under chapter 302.
38 6. Golf carts shall not be operated on sidewalks constructed for pedestrian use or
39 on trails designated for pedestrians and bicyclists.
40 7. Where a path for golf carts is designated and available for use that is adjacent
41 to a highway or street, a person operating a golf cart may, by local ordinance, be
42 required to use that designated path.
43 8. A person operating a golf cart shall drive the vehicle as near to the right side
44 of the roadway as practicable.
45 9. A person operating or riding in a golf cart shall not be required to wear
46 protective headgear.
47 10. As used in this section, a "golf cart" means a motor vehicle that is designed and
48 manufactured for operation on a golf course for sporting or recreational purposes and that is
49 not capable of exceeding speeds of [twenty] twenty-five miles per hour.
